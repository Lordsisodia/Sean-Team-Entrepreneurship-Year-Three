### Improvement #2: Make Year 3 Connections Specific, Not Aspirational

**Priority:** 2 (Medium Impact)
**Expected Grade Impact:** +5-8%
**Pattern Addressed:** Specific Application vs Generic Intent

**Current Issue:**
The "Connections to Year 3" section states what will be done but not HOW it will be applied. For 90-100%, connections should show specific application plans, not just intent statements.

**Location:**
"CONNECTIONS TO YEAR 3" section (bottom of document)

**Current Content (excerpt):**
```
**Innovation Management → Service Design & Delivery (Year 3)**
Confronting my perfectionism-as-avoidance pattern enables me to actually execute partnership work, not just design partnership frameworks. The self-awareness question—"Is this system serving our goals, or am I hiding in technical work?"—will be crucial for balancing design with execution.
```

**Problem:** "will be crucial" is aspirational, not specific.

**Suggested Change:**
Rewrite each connection to include:
1. Specific Year 3 project/situation where it will apply
2. Concrete action to take (not just awareness)
3. Success metric or observable behavior

**Example transformation:**
```
**Innovation Management → Service Design & Delivery (Year 3)**
When developing the agency partnership model in Q1-Q2, I will track weekly how much time I spend on framework design versus direct partner outreach. If design work exceeds 40% of my time, I will force-switch to outreach activities for the remainder of the week, using the question "Is this system serving our goals, or am I hiding?" as my checkpoint.
```

**Implementation Guidance for Worker Agent:**
1. Read each "Connection to Year 3" statement
2. Identify the specific Year 3 work referenced (check Q4 plan if needed)
3. Add: specific project context, concrete action, observable metric
4. Keep the same competency-to-competency mapping
5. Ensure each connection is 2-3 sentences maximum

**Success Criteria:**
- [ ] Each connection names specific Year 3 project/work
- [ ] Each connection includes concrete action (not just awareness)
- [ ] Each connection has observable metric or behavior
- [ ] Total word count for this section reduced (more specific = fewer words)

### Improvement #5: Strengthen Self-Management Pattern Connection

**Priority:** 5 (Lowest Impact)
**Expected Grade Impact:** +2-3%
**Pattern Addressed:** Pattern Coherence

**Current Issue:**
Self-Management competency identifies two potentially contradictory patterns without reconciling them: (1) thriving under pressure, and (2) resisting delegation due to "wanting to understand the process." These are presented separately but could be connected for deeper insight.

**Location:**
Competency 3: Self-Management, third paragraph

**Current Content (excerpt):**
```
However, this pattern conflicts with another behaviour I've been developing: when I delegate tasks to AI agents or team members, I often resist because I "want to understand the process" myself. I say this is about quality control, but when I'm honest with myself, it's often about discomfort with not knowing exactly how work is being done. This tension—thriving under pressure I create myself while simultaneously resisting delegation due to control needs—creates a complicated self-management challenge.
```

**Analysis:** This identifies the tension but doesn't explore the connection: both patterns may be about CONTROL.

**Suggested Change:**
Connect the patterns more explicitly:

```
Reflecting on this, I see a connection between my urgency addiction and my delegation resistance: both are about control. I create crisis-mode pressure because it gives me a clear enemy (the deadline) to fight against, and I resist delegation because I lose control over process. When I'm honest with myself, I'm not addicted to urgency—I'm addicted to certainty and control. This insight reframes my Year 3 target: it's not just about developing consistency without crisis, it's about becoming comfortable with uncertainty—whether that's uncertain outcomes through delegation or uncertain workflows without artificial deadlines.
```

**Implementation Guidance for Worker Agent:**
1. Read the Self-Management competency
2. Identify the two patterns presented
3. Find the underlying connection (control)
4. Rewrite to show this deeper insight
5. Keep it to 100-150 words total

**Success Criteria:**
- [ ] Patterns explicitly connected (not just juxtaposed)
- [ ] Deeper insight revealed (control = root cause)
- [ ] Word count maintained or reduced
- [ ] Connection to Year 3 strengthened by this insight
- [ ] Personal reflection tone enhanced
