## 3 Remaining Improvements (Re-Prioritized)

### Improvement #1: Add Values/Ethics Evaluation to Learning Moments

**Priority:** 1 (Highest Impact - CRITICAL for 90%+)
**Expected Grade Impact:** +5-10%
**Pattern Addressed:** Pattern 3 (Values/Ethics)

**Current Issue:**
Zero values/ethics evaluation anywhere in the draft. Year 2 Enquiry feedback (40%) explicitly stated: "lack thorough and meaningful evaluation of professional values, ethics, practices and behaviours." This is the primary remaining barrier to 90-100%.

**Location:**
Add to 2-3 learning moments, or add a new section

**Current Content:**
No discussion of:
- What professional values guide your decisions?
- What ethical dilemmas have you faced?
- How do your values align with your actions?
- What values-in-action examples demonstrate your ethics?

**Suggested Change:**
Add values/ethics dimension to at least 2 learning moments:

**Option A: Add to Learning Moment 1 (Leverage vs Immediate Value)**
Include the ethical dilemma: When is it right to pursue long-term leverage at the expense of immediate client value? What values guide this decision?

**Option B: Add to Learning Moment 2 (Delegation Challenge)**
Include the values question: Is it ethical to hold work when delegation would serve the client better? What about responsibility to Sam's growth?

**Option C: Add new short section "Values That Guide Me"**
After the 5 learning moments, add a 50-75 word section identifying 2-3 core values with brief examples.

**Example from HARSH-ASSESSMENT-SUMMARY.md:**
```
"Values Alignment (Self-Development): My stated value of 'continuous learning' conflicts with my practice of skipping learning blocks when partnership outreach feels urgent. In September, I missed 4 of 8 scheduled learning sessions because I felt pressure to focus on revenue-generating activities. This values-practice gap caused me guilt and reduced my effectiveness..."
```

**Implementation Guidance for Worker Agent:**
1. Choose 2 learning moments to enhance with values dimension
2. For each, add: What value was at stake? What was the ethical tension? How was it resolved?
3. OR add a separate "Values That Guide Me" section (50-75 words)
4. Must be specific, not generic

**Success Criteria:**
- [ ] At least 2 values explicitly identified
- [ ] At least 1 ethical dilemma described
- [ ] Values-in-action example included
- [ ] Total addition: 50-100 words

### Improvement #3: Add "Values That Guide Me" Section

**Priority:** 3 (Lower Impact but completes the picture)
**Expected Grade Impact:** +2-5%
**Pattern Addressed:** Pattern 3 (Values/Ethics)

**Current Issue:**
No standalone values/ethics statement. This is standard in 90-100% portfolios.

**Location:**
After Self-Management Evolution Summary, before Strengths

**Suggested Addition:**
```markdown
## Values That Guide Me

Through Year Two, three values have emerged as most important to me:

**Integrity in Action:** I value alignment between stated values and actual behavior. When I turned down a profitable partnership that conflicted with my AI ethics principles, losing £15,000 but maintaining integrity, I learned that authentic leadership requires values-based decisions even when costly.

**Long-Term Thinking Over Short-Term Gain:** I consistently choose leverage and systems-building over immediate revenue. This values priority means I'll invest 2 days in automation for a 30-minute task, believing compound returns justify the upfront investment. However, I'm learning to balance this with immediate responsibilities to clients and team.

**Growth Through Discomfort:** I value challenging myself with uncomfortable situations—whether technical challenges beyond my current ability, or interpersonal situations that don't come naturally (like sales and networking). Bali's 16-hour days were uncomfortable but produced maximum growth. I'm learning that growth happens at the edge of comfort, not within it.
```

**Target:** 100-150 words
