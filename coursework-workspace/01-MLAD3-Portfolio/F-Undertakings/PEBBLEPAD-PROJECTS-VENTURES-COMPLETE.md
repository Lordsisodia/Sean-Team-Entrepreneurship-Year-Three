## Project/Venture 13: Restaurant SaaS - Automation Apps

**Brief description:**
Designed and built restaurant automation systems including ordering platforms, menu management, and payment integration. Developed restaurant-specific templates and vertical expertise. Targeted restaurant owners as primary customer segment with white-label app solutions. Built full-featured MVP for paying client including ordering system, payment processing, and mobile app architecture.

**Pre-Project Review:**
- Assumptions: Restaurants would be ideal automation clients due to clear operational needs (ordering, payments, inventory)
- Market Validation: Limited - based on general industry trends rather than direct customer research
- Resources Allocated: 6+ weeks of development time, template creation, and collateral development

- What Worked: Technical execution was excellent - delivered functional, satisfied product to client. Payment integration, mobile app architecture, and order management systems all functioned correctly.
- What Didn't Work: Business model was fundamentally flawed. Client feedback revealed: restaurants lack capital, have tight margins, low customer volume, and automation wouldn't generate significant additional revenue.

- Outcome: Successful technical delivery, strategic failure. Led to pivot to e-commerce segment where clients have revenue, capital, and automation ROI.

## Project/Venture 15: Crypto SaaS - Difficult Client Experience

**Brief description:**
Took on crypto app development project for demanding client. Client was aggressive in communication, expected immediate responses at all hours, frequently changed requirements, and was unreliable with payments. After 3 weeks of escalating difficulty, ended the client relationship. Project was incomplete but provided valuable learning about client selection and qualification.

**Pre-Project Review:**
- Decision-Making: Accepted project despite early red flags (demanding tone, urgency) due to revenue attraction and desire to prove technical capabilities
- Assumptions: All revenue is good revenue; difficult clients can be managed through better communication and boundaries
- Risk Assessment: Insufficient - prioritized short-term revenue over long-term sustainability

**Interim/Post-Project Review:**
- What Went Wrong:
  - Client was difficult from first conversation - red flags (aggressive tone, urgency, payment delays) were present from week one
  - Attempted to maintain professional boundaries while client pushed back against reasonable constraints
  - Consumed disproportionate time, energy, and emotional bandwidth relative to revenue provided
- Key Learning: "Client selection happens before project commitment, not during. You can't turn a difficult client into an easy one."
- Second Insight: "Difficult clients consume more than time - they consume mental energy and emotional bandwidth." This creates opportunity costs with better clients.
- Process Changes: Developed client qualification framework: respectful communication, reasonable expectations, payment reliability, clear requirements.
- Outcome: Project incomplete, but lesson was valuable. Learned that saying no to bad revenue creates space for ideal clients.

**Date started:** November 2025
**Date ended:** November 2025 (3 weeks duration, terminated)

**Current State:**
- Project terminated (difficult client)
- Client qualification framework developed
- Informed future client selection strategies

- Outcome: Ongoing development. Infrastructure operational and being integrated into client/partner offerings.
