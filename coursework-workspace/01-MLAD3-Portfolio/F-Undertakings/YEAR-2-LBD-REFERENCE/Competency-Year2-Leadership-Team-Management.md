## Competency

**What is the name and definition of this competency?**

Leadership & Team Management: The ability to inspire, guide, and manage a team towards achieving common goals, while ensuring alignment with the company's vision.

**How is this competency relevant to my situation and SMART Objectives?**

Leadership and team management are critical to the successful execution of my Year 2 goals, particularly the ambitious objective of scaling the AI Influencer project from 6 influencers to over 100 by the end of the year. This expansion requires not only strategic thinking but also the ability to manage a larger, more diverse team effectively. As the project lead, I must ensure that all team members are aligned with the company's vision and are working towards common goals.

Effective leadership in this context involves clear communication of objectives, setting realistic but challenging goals, and providing the necessary support and resources for my team to succeed. Additionally, managing a growing team means being able to delegate tasks efficiently, ensuring that each member is contributing to their fullest potential. This competency is also vital in creating a positive team culture, where collaboration is encouraged, and everyone feels motivated and valued.

Moreover, this competency is directly linked to my SMART objectives, such as increasing active engagement within the team entrepreneurship community and establishing a mentoring program. Both of these goals require strong leadership to foster collaboration and guide less experienced team members. By honing my leadership and team management skills, I will be better equipped to drive the project's success, contribute to the personal development of my team members, and achieve the broader objectives of my role.

## Key Learning

One of the most significant learning moments in my development of leadership and team management skills occurred during the onboarding process of new interns. Initially, I assumed that my instructions were clear and that the team would naturally understand the project's goals. However, I quickly realized that assumptions and unclear communication can lead to misunderstandings and delays. A particular incident where a miscommunication caused a two-week delay in project timelines was a wake-up call. This experience taught me the importance of setting clear expectations from the outset and ensuring that all team members are on the same page. To address this, I implemented regular feedback loops and clearer guidelines, which significantly improved team alignment and productivity.

Another critical learning moment came during a period of intense pressure when we were facing a tight deadline for a high-stakes project. The team was overwhelmed, and morale was low, which threatened our ability to deliver on time. As the project lead, I had to step up and take decisive action. I quickly organized a series of motivational meetings, where I reassured the team of our capabilities, reminded them of our previous successes, and outlined a clear, achievable plan to meet the deadline. This approach not only boosted the team's morale but also refocused their efforts, allowing us to meet the deadline with high-quality results. This situation underscored the value of leadership in maintaining focus, energy, and commitment during challenging times.

Furthermore, a valuable learning experience came from leading a cross-functional team on a collaborative project. The team comprised individuals from diverse backgrounds, each with different working styles and communication preferences. Initially, this diversity led to conflicts and misunderstandings, which hindered our progress. To resolve this, I adapted my leadership style, embracing a more inclusive approach that encouraged open communication and leveraged the strengths of each team member. By fostering an environment where everyone felt heard and valued, we were able to turn our differences into a source of strength, ultimately delivering a more innovative and successful project than any of us could have achieved alone.

These experiences have significantly shaped my leadership and team management competency. They taught me that leadership is not just about directing others but also about creating an environment where people can thrive, feel motivated, and work together towards common goals. The ability to communicate clearly, adapt to the needs of the team, and maintain morale under pressure are now key aspects of my leadership approach, and they have been instrumental in the success of my projects.

## Progression through Practice

My leadership and team management skills have significantly progressed through hands-on experiences in various projects and ventures within the team company. One of the most impactful areas of growth was during the onboarding of new interns. I took a proactive approach, personally guiding them through the company's processes, ensuring they felt supported and understood their roles. This hands-on method allowed me to identify individual strengths and weaknesses early on, which informed how I delegated tasks and responsibilities. For example, I noticed that some interns excelled in creative tasks, while others were more analytical. By tailoring their roles to their strengths, I was able to maximize productivity and enhance their learning experiences.

In managing several critical projects, I applied leadership skills daily. I organized and led regular one-on-one meetings with team members, not only to track progress but also to understand their professional growth needs and how I could support them. These meetings were pivotal in building trust and maintaining open lines of communication. They allowed me to provide timely feedback, address any issues before they escalated, and adjust project plans as needed. This iterative process of feedback and adjustment helped the team stay aligned with our objectives and improved overall project outcomes.

One of the most challenging yet rewarding experiences was leading the expansion of our operations, particularly the AI Influencer project. This expansion required me to make critical decisions under pressure, resolve conflicts within the team, and maintain high levels of motivation despite tight deadlines. For instance, when we faced technical setbacks, I had to quickly rally the team, reassign resources, and develop a contingency plan. This situation tested my ability to lead under pressure and reinforced the importance of adaptability in leadership. It also highlighted the necessity of being decisive yet inclusive, ensuring that the team felt involved in the decision-making process, which in turn boosted their commitment to the project.

Additionally, managing cross-functional teams across different projects provided me with opportunities to practice conflict resolution. Diverse teams often come with varying perspectives, which, while valuable, can sometimes lead to disagreements. I took the initiative to mediate conflicts by fostering a culture of open communication and mutual respect. By encouraging team members to voice their concerns and facilitating constructive discussions, I was able to turn potential conflicts into opportunities for collaboration and innovation. This experience honed my ability to manage team dynamics effectively, ensuring that conflicts were resolved in a way that strengthened the team rather than dividing it.

Practicing leadership and team management in these real-world scenarios has significantly contributed to my overall development. It has taught me the importance of being adaptable, empathetic, and decisive. Moreover, it has reinforced the idea that effective leadership is not about control but about empowering others to achieve their best. As a result, I have become more confident in my ability to lead complex projects and manage diverse teams, skills that will be crucial as I continue to pursue my strategic objectives.

## Progression through Enquiry

Over the past year, enquiry-based learning has been instrumental in enhancing my leadership and team management competencies. Engaging with various resources, including books, workshops, and online courses, has provided me with valuable insights that have shaped my leadership approach.

A key influence has been Transformational Leadership, which emphasizes inspiring and motivating team members to achieve more than they thought possible. This theory encouraged me to adopt a more visionary leadership style, focusing on aligning my team's goals with a broader mission and fostering a culture of innovation. Understanding transformational leadership has helped me move beyond simply managing tasks to empowering my team to take initiative and think creatively.

I also explored Situational Leadership, which advocates for adapting leadership styles based on the needs and maturity levels of team members. This model has been particularly useful in managing a diverse team, allowing me to be more directive when necessary and more hands-off when team members are capable of taking the lead. This adaptability has been key in creating an environment where all team members feel supported and capable.

Another important concept I delved into was Emotional Intelligence (EI). Learning about EI highlighted the importance of self-awareness, empathy, and relationship management in leadership. These skills have been crucial in resolving conflicts, maintaining team morale, and building stronger relationships with team members. By applying EI principles, I have been able to lead with a greater understanding of both my own emotions and those of my team, which has enhanced our collaboration and productivity.

Workshops on Communication Strategies further enriched my understanding of effective leadership. I learned that clear and consistent communication is vital for setting expectations and providing feedback. Developing my active listening skills has also been a focus, enabling me to better understand my team's concerns and ideas, which has, in turn, improved my ability to manage the team effectively.

Finally, enquiry-based learning has deepened my understanding of Leadership Ethics. Discussions on ethical leadership reinforced the importance of integrity, transparency, and fairness in decision-making. These principles have guided my actions as a leader, ensuring that I lead in a way that is not only effective but also ethically sound. This commitment to ethical leadership has helped build trust within my team, as they know that my decisions are guided by strong ethical considerations.

In summary, the theories and concepts I have engaged with through enquiry-based learning have significantly influenced my approach to leadership. By integrating transformational leadership, situational leadership, emotional intelligence, and ethical principles into my practice, I have developed a more holistic and effective leadership style. This deeper understanding has enabled me to lead my team with greater confidence and adaptability, preparing me for more complex leadership challenges in the future.
