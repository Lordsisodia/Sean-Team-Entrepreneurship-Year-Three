## SECTION 3: KEY LEARNING (Max 500 words)

### Learning Moment 1: Pricing > Cost-Plus (November 2025)
**Context:** Setting partnership pricing model
**What Happened:** Initially considered cost-plus pricing (calculate costs, add margin). Shifted to value-based pricing (what partners willing to pay)
**Discovery Learned:** Pricing based on customer value, not business costs. £500/month represents 10x ROI for partners (minimum £5K/month value delivered)
**Specific Outcome:** 12-15% conversion rate at £500/month proves value-based pricing works
**How Understanding Changed:** Financial management isn't about covering costs—it's about capturing value created

### Learning Moment 2: Unit Economics Validation (April 2026)
**Context:** Achieving 100-partner milestone
**What Happened:** Reached 102 partners generating £51K MRR. Validated unit economics: CAC < LTV, positive gross margin
**Discovery Learned:** Business model validation requires actual data, not projections. 102 partners proved unit economics work at scale
**Specific Outcome:** Confident to target 500 partners (£250K/month) because model validated
**How Understanding Changed:** Evidence-based financial decisions prevent speculative scaling (unlike Year 2's internal app mistake)

### Learning Moment 3: Revenue Funds Growth (January 2026)
**Context:** Making first partnership manager hire
**What Happened:** £50K MRR from 100 partners enabled hiring Sam as partnership manager. Revenue funds team expansion
**Discovery Learned:** Sustainable scaling requires revenue funding, not external investment. Bootstrap approach forces unit economics discipline
**Specific Outcome:** Hired first team member January 2026 without external capital
**How Understanding Changed:** Financial management enables strategic team building—revenue validates business, hires fund growth

## SECTION 4: PROGRESSION THROUGH PRACTICE (Max 500 words)

### Practice 1: Pricing Model Development (September - November 2025)
**Timeline:** September 2025 (research) → November 2025 (validation)
**People Involved:** Test partners, competitors (pricing analysis)
**Activity:** Researched market pricing, tested different price points, validated with actual partners
**Putting Competency into Practice:**
- Analyzed competitor pricing: £200-£1,000/month range
- Conducted willingness-to-pay surveys with 20 prospects
- Tested £300 vs £500 vs £700 price points
- Calculated partner ROI: Minimum £5K/month value delivered
**Measurable Outcomes:**
- Selected £500/month as optimal price (balance of conversion and revenue)
- 12-15% conversion rate at £500 (vs 20% at £300, 5% at £700)
- Validated value-based pricing approach
**Impact on Development:** Proved pricing is strategic decision, not calculation—set foundation for £50K MRR

### Practice 2: Unit Economics Analysis (December 2025 - March 2026)
**Timeline:** December 2025 (analysis) → March 2026 (projections)
**People Involved:** Mentors, SaaS founders, financial advisors
**Activity:** Analyzed unit economics, modeled scaling scenarios, validated assumptions
**Putting Competency into Practice:**
- Calculated Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC): ~£250 per partner
- Calculated Customer Lifetime Value (LTV): £6,000+ (12+ month average partnership)
- CAC:LTV ratio: 1:24 (healthy, < 1:3 threshold)
- Modeled 100, 250, 500 partner scenarios
**Measurable Outcomes:**
- Confirmed unit economics support 500-partner scale
- 500-partner projection: £250K/month revenue
- Identified breakeven: 25 partners cover all operational costs
**Impact on Development:** Evidence-based scaling decision—validated 100-partner model before 500-partner commitment

### Practice 3: Revenue Operations Management (October 2025 - April 2026)

## SECTION 5: PROGRESSION THROUGH ENQUIRY (Max 500 words)

### Framework 1: Value-Based Pricing (Learned October - November 2025)
**Source:** LfO-05 (Financial Thinking & Business Models), pricing strategy research
**Key Insights:**
- Price based on value delivered to customer, not cost to produce
- ROI justification enables premium pricing
- Pricing power indicates product-market fit
**How Applied:** Analyzed partner ROI (minimum £5K/month value), priced at £500/month (10x ROI)
**Impact:** 12-15% conversion rate proves value-based pricing works

### Framework 2: Unit Economics & CAC:LTV (Learned December 2025 - January 2026)
**Source:** SaaS metrics research, investor presentations, mentor discussions
**Key Insights:**
- CAC:LTV ratio of 1:3+ indicates healthy business model
- Payback period < 12 months ensures positive cash flow
- Gross margin > 80% enables sustainable scaling
**How Applied:** Calculated CAC (£250), LTV (£6,000+), ratio 1:24. Confirmed 500-partner viability
**Impact:** Evidence-based scaling decision, confident in £250K/month projection

### Framework 3: Bootstrap vs Venture Funding (Learned January - February 2026)
**Source:** Founder case studies, TE alumni discussions, startup finance research
**Key Insights:**
- Bootstrap forces unit economics discipline
- Revenue-funded growth slower but more sustainable
- External capital enables speed but dilutes ownership
**How Applied:** Chose bootstrap path—used £50K MRR to fund first hire rather than raising investment
**Impact:** Maintained full ownership, validated business model before scaling

### Framework 4: SaaS Metrics & Churn Analysis (Learned February - March 2026)
**Source:** SaaS benchmarking reports, industry metrics, founder forums
**Key Insights:**
- MRR growth, churn rate, expansion revenue are key health indicators
- <5% monthly churn is healthy for B2B SaaS
- Negative churn (expansion > churn) drives exponential growth
**How Applied:** Tracked MRR growth (£0→£51K), measured churn (<5%), monitored expansion revenue
**Impact:** Data-driven business decisions, early warning system for issues
