## Competency

**What is the name and definition of this competency?**

Self-Management: The ability to regulate one's emotions, thoughts, and behaviors effectively, including managing stress, maintaining discipline, building sustainable habits, and developing personal capacity to enable professional success. This encompasses habit formation, time management, energy management, and honest self-assessment.

**How is this competency relevant to my situation and SMART Objectives?**

Self-management is the foundation that enables all other competencies. SMART Objective 1 (80%+ cannabis-free days, 50+ focused hours/week) and Objective 2 (time-tracking system for capacity planning) both target self-management capability. The 40% and 85% progress ratings respectively reflect both progress and the gap between intention and execution.

This competency is critical because partnership scaling (100→500 partners) demands sustained high performance. Self-management isn't a nice-to-have—it's the enabling constraint on what I can achieve. The connection to Year 2 is significant: in Year 2, I successfully quit smoking through habit change principles, demonstrating that I could design systems for personal transformation. In Year 3, I'm applying those same principles to professional capacity.

## Key Learning

One significant learning moment came from the time-tracking app data (October 2025 onwards). I had assumed I was maintaining good habits—waking up early, working focused hours, staying cannabis-free. The app revealed a different reality: ~50% actual compliance vs the 100% target I believed I was hitting. This was confronting but valuable. The insight was: "Honest self-assessment requires data, not perception. We systematically overestimate our own adherence to habits." This led to adjusting the cannabis-free target from 100% to 80% and the focused hours target from 70 to 50 per week.

Another key learning came from recognising the difference between maximum capacity and sustainable capacity. In October-November 2025, I attempted 70+ focused hours per week. I experienced early signs of burnout—diminishing returns, fatigue, reduced quality. The insight was: "Self-management = knowing sustainable limits, not maximising to exhaustion. Long-term health > short-term achievement." This led to adjusting to 50 focused hours/week.

A third insight emerged from the 6:30 AM wake routine practice. I established this routine in October 2025. The reality has been 80%+ consistency. The learning was: "Consistent morning routines create foundation for productive days, but perfection isn't required. 80% consistency creates most of the value."

A fourth learning moment came from connecting Year 2 smoking cessation to Year 3 habit management. In Year 2, I successfully quit smoking through environmental design and systematic tracking. In Year 3, I'm applying the same principles: time-tracking app for self-awareness, 6:30 AM routine for environmental consistency, realistic targets for sustainability. The insight was: "The same habit formation principles apply to professional capacity. Systems design > willpower."

These experiences collectively shaped my understanding of self-management. I moved from viewing self-management as willpower and discipline to viewing it as systems design and honest data.

## Progression through Enquiry

Enquiry-based learning contributed to my self-management development through understanding the science of habit formation and capacity management.

Key frameworks studied:

**Atomic Habits (James Clear):** 1% better daily = 37x improvement in a year. Habits form through consistency, not intensity. 80% consistency creates most of the value. Habits stack—attaching new habits to existing routines.

**Circadian Rhythms Research:** Energy levels follow predictable patterns. Deep work best during peak energy hours (morning). Sleep consistency foundational. This influenced the 6:30 AM routine and scheduling deep work in mornings.

**Deep Work and Flow State Research:** Focused work is rare and valuable. 50 focused hours/week of deep work more valuable than 70 hours of shallow work. This influenced capacity planning—prioritising deep work over shallow work.

**Burnout Prevention Research:** Burnout is preventable. Sustainable capacity < maximum capacity. Recovery as important as work. This influenced the 70→50 hour adjustment.

**Habit Formation Science:** 21-66 days to form habits. Missing one day doesn't break habit, consistency over time matters. 80% consistency sufficient for most benefits.

How this influenced my professional values and practices:

Self-management research reinforced commitment to sustainability over intensity. Old approach: maximum effort, burnout, recover. New approach: sustainable capacity, consistent habits, long-term performance.

I also developed appreciation for systems over willpower. The time-tracking app, morning routine, honest target setting are systems that make success easier.

By integrating this knowledge, I've built self-management systems that support my goals. The time-tracking app (85% progress), 6:30 AM routine, and adjusted targets all reflect this.

However, there's still a gap: 40% progress on Objective 1 reflects actual ~50% compliance vs 80% target. Closing this gap requires continued focus.
