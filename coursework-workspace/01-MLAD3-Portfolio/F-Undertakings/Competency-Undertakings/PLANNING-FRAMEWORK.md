## Planning Framework for Each Competency

For each competency undertaking, plan by answering these questions:

### 1. Where am I now? (Current Level - 50-75 words)
- What specific gap or weakness exists?
- What evidence shows this current level?
- Be honest and specific

### 2. Where do I want to be? (Target Level - context throughout)
- What does "good" look like for this competency?
- What specific capability am I building?
- How will I measure progress?

### 3. What did I do? (Action Taken - 150-200 words)
- **Specific activities** undertaken
- **Timeline** - when did this happen?
- **Resources used** - mentorship, reading, courses, practice
- **People involved** - Sam, TE coach, team members, mentors

### 4. What did I learn? (Critical Evaluation - 200-250 words)
- **Insights gained** - what challenged assumptions?
- **Surprises** - what was unexpected?
- **Challenges faced** - what was difficult?
- **What would I do differently?** - honest reflection
- **Failures and mistakes** - include these!

### 5. How do I know I developed? (Learning Achieved - 100-125 words)
- **Specific skills** developed
- **Measurable progress** - what improved?
- **Connection to objectives** - which SMART objective does this support?
- **Impact** - what changed in ventures/projects/team?

### 6. What's next? (Future Application - 50-75 words)
- **Continued development** - what needs more work?
- **Next milestone** - what's the specific next step?
- **Long-term application** - how will this support post-TE goals?

## Quality Checklist for Each Undertaking

Before submitting each competency undertaking, verify:

### Content Quality
- [ ] ~500-600 words total
- [ ] Explicit connection to the specific competency
- [ ] Honest assessment of current level (not overstating)
- [ ] Specific development activities (not vague)
- [ ] Critical evaluation depth (not just description)
- [ ] Concrete application examples
- [ ] Measurable progress or outcomes
- [ ] Connection to SMART Objectives where relevant

### Reflection Quality
- [ ] What challenged assumptions is addressed
- [ ] What surprised you is included
- [ ] What would you do differently is stated
- [ ] Failures and mistakes acknowledged
- [ ] Limitations and gaps identified
- [ ] Future development needs clear

### Integration Quality
- [ ] Links to Learning Contract (Section 08)
- [ ] Connections to other competencies noted
- [ ] References to LfO/LbD undertakings where applicable
- [ ] Values & Ethics dimensions addressed (for Academic Rigor & Critical Thinking)

### Writing Quality
- [ ] Clear, professional language
- [ ] Appropriate word count per section
- [ ] Logical structure and flow
- [ ] Specific examples and details
- [ ] No fluff or filler

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

### ❌ Don't do this:
- Vague statements like "I improved my communication"
- Ignoring failures and only mentioning successes
- Overstating current skill level
- Disconnected from actual experiences
- No specific examples or evidence
- Superficial reflection without depth

### ✅ Do this instead:
- Specific statements like "Team adoption increased from 40% to 65% when I..."
- Honest discussion of what didn't work and why
- Candid assessment of current weaknesses
- Grounded in real experiences and activities
- Concrete examples with dates, numbers, details
- Deep reflection on assumptions, challenges, and growth
