## 🎯 WHAT ARE COMPETENCY UNDERTAKINGS?

**From PebblePad structure:**
- **Competency Undertakings** are separate assets from the Learning Contract
- You create 6 separate competency undertaking forms (one per competency)
- You link to these in the "Competency Record" tab of your Personal Portfolio
- **Format:** Same template as Year 2 (Context, Progression, Key Learning, Practice, Enquiry, Next Steps)

**Year 2 Example Structure:**
1. Competency name & definition
2. Relevance to situation & SMART objectives (200 words max)
3. Progression summary (300 words max)
4. Key learning moments (500 words max)
5. Progression through practice (500 words max)
6. Progression through enquiry (500 words max)
7. Next steps (300 words max)

**Total per competency:** ~2,200 words (vs 500-600 in Learning Contract)

## 📊 FIRST PRINCIPLES: WHAT ARE WE PROVING?

### **The Core Question:**
**"How did I develop this competency from Year 2 to Year 3?"**

Each competency undertaking must demonstrate:
1. **Year 2 baseline** - Where you started (from Year 2 coursework)
2. **Year 3 actions** - What you actually did (with evidence)
3. **Critical evaluation** - What worked, what didn't, what you'd change
4. **Measurable progression** - Specific numbers, dates, outcomes
5. **Connection to theory** - Frameworks applied (Hersey-Blanchard, Gibb's, etc.)
6. **Future application** - How you'll continue developing

### **What Makes It OUTSTANDING (90-100%)?**

**From marking criteria analysis:**
- **Significant effort evident** - not minimal work
- **In-depth critical evaluation** - not just description
- **High self-awareness** - honest assessment, not overstating
- **Personal accountability** - owning mistakes, not blaming others
- **Specific, tangible application** - real examples with numbers/dates
- **Insightful connections** - theory to practice, Year 2 to Year 3

**The 90-100% Differentiator:**
- **70% (First Class):** "Here's what I did, here's what I learned"
- **90% (Outstanding):** "Here's what I did, here's WHY it worked/didn't work, here's what I'd do differently, here's how I changed as a person"

## 📝 PEBBLEPAD COMPETENCY UNDERTAKING STRUCTURE

### **Section 1: Competency (Definition + Relevance)**
**Word count:** ~200 words max

**What to include:**
- Competency name and definition
- Relevance to Year 3 situation
- Connection to SMART objectives
- Why critical for your exit strategy

**Template:**
```
[Competency Name]: [Definition]

How is this competency relevant to my situation and SMART Objectives?
[2-3 paragraphs explaining relevance to Year 3 goals, specific SMART objectives connected, and why critical for success]
```

---

### **Section 2: Progression Summary**
**Word count:** ~300 words max

**What to include:**
- Year 2 starting level (Foundation/Intermediate/Advanced)
- Why you rated yourself at that level
- Year 3 level reached
- Was this in line with expectations?
- Accuracy of initial self-evaluation
- Were you ambitious enough?
- Key factors contributing to progression

**Template:**
```
At the beginning of Year 3, I assessed my [competency] skills at [level].
- Year 2 baseline: [where you ended Year 2]
- Year 3 target: [what you aimed to achieve]

Over Year 3, I progressed to [level], driven by [key factors].
My initial assessment was [accurate/conservative/ambitious] because [explanation].

Key factors that contributed to my progression include [3-4 specific factors with evidence].
```

---

### **Section 3: Key Learning**
**Word count:** ~500 words max

**What to include:**
- 2-3 significant learning moments
- What you discovered/realized
- How this changed your understanding
- Specific examples with dates
- Connection to competency development

**Template:**
```
One significant learning moment occurred when [describe situation with date].
This taught me [key insight]. I learned that [lesson].

Another key learning came from [describe situation].
This experience reinforced [principle]. The specific outcome: [measurable result].

These experiences shaped my [competency] development by [how you changed].
```

---

### **Section 4: Progression through Practice**
**Word count:** ~500 words max

**What to include:**
- Specific projects/ventures/activities
- How you put competency into practice
- Timeline (when things happened)
- People involved
- Measurable outcomes
- Impact on overall development

**Template:**
```
I progressed in [competency] through hands-on practice in [specific projects].

One major practice: [describe activity with timeline].
I [specific action]. This resulted in [measurable outcome].

Another key practice: [describe activity].
I [specific action]. The impact: [measurable result].

Practicing this competency contributed to my overall development by [how you grew].
```

---

### **Section 5: Progression through Enquiry**
**Word count:** ~500 words max

**What to include:**
- Theories/models/frameworks learned
- Key insights from different sources
- Comparing contrasting perspectives
- How values/ethics influenced
- How knowledge enabled more effective practice

**Template:**
```
Enquiry-based learning was instrumental in developing my [competency].
I engaged with [resources: books, courses, workshops, research].

Key frameworks studied:
- [Framework 1] - [how it influenced you]
- [Framework 2] - [how it influenced you]

Key insights from different sources:
- [Source 1] taught me [insight]
- [Source 2] provided [perspective]

How this influenced my professional values/practices:
- [Value/ethics connection]

By integrating this knowledge, I've been able to [apply more effectively].
```

---

### **Section 6: Next Steps**
**Word count:** ~300 words max

**What to include:**
- How to continue developing
- Specific next milestones
- Application to projects/ventures
- Professional/self development
- Long-term post-TE goals

**Template:**
```
Moving forward, I plan to develop [competency] by [specific actions].

I will [specific next steps] by [timeline].

This will support my [projects/ventures] by [how].

Long-term, this competency will enable [post-TE goals].
```
