## Key Learning

One significant learning moment came from the restaurant→ecommerce pivot (October-November 2025). After investing 6+ weeks in restaurant templates and expertise, client feedback revealed that restaurants lacked capital and revenue potential.

The profound insight: **"Client feedback about their business situation is market intelligence, not just conversation. If one restaurant client has these economics, others likely do too."**

When the restaurant client explained their constraints - low customer volume, tight margins, limited capital - this wasn't just chat. It was data about an entire market segment. The pivot decision required overriding emotional attachment to invested work (sunk cost fallacy) and following the economic evidence.

Another key learning came from the difficult crypto client experience (LbD-05). The client was demanding, aggressive, and failed to pay on time. After 3 weeks, I ended the relationship. The learning: **"Not all revenue is good revenue. Client selection happens before project commitment, not during."**

I had ignored early red flags (demanding tone, urgency) because I wanted the revenue. The experience taught a qualification framework: Do they respect professional boundaries? Are they reasonable about timelines? Can they pay on time? Do I feel energised or drained after talking to them?

A third insight emerged from the construction deal collapse (LbD-06). The deal with Nick Mearson faded without clear rejection. I felt disappointed initially but reframed: **"Deal fallout is normal, not personal. Rejection isn't about competence - it's about fit, timing, and circumstances I can't control."**

The collapse revealed I was over-invested in single opportunities. I learned to maintain multiple deals in parallel so no single loss creates crisis. Pipeline diversification is emotional protection as much as business strategy.
