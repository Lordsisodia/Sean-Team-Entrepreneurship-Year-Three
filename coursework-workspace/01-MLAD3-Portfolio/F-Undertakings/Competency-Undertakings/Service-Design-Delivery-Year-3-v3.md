## Key Learning

One significant learning moment occurred during the Restaurant Ordering App build (October 10-18, 2025, documented in Activity Log). I had built the technical features—menu management, order tracking, payment integration—but I realised I was building a product, not designing a service. The restaurant owner needed more than software; they needed the service system around it: how customers order, how staff are trained, how orders are fulfilled. This forced me to think beyond features to service journey design. I created a reusable template that other Bali restaurants could use. This taught me that "service design encompasses the entire customer experience, not just the software product. Templates are service systems."

Another key learning came from the systematic documentation effort itself. The ENHANCED-ACTIVITY-LOG-ENTRIES.md file grew to 2,289 lines through sustained work from October 2025 through January 2026. What surprised me was how documentation clarity improves service design. Writing detailed activity descriptions forced me to think through the service journey step-by-step. The learning was: "Documentation isn't just reporting what happened—it's a design tool. Writing clearly about service experiences reveals gaps and opportunities that intuitive thinking misses."

A third insight emerged from reviewing the 6 LbD undertakings I created. Looking at files like LbD-04-Delegation-to-Sam.md (12,473 bytes) and LbD-06-Construction-Deal-Collapse.md (11,974 bytes), I realised that the most detailed undertakings were the ones where I had invested in systematic service design upfront. The lean undertakings were ones where I had jumped straight to execution. The retrospective learning was: "Service design investment upfront correlates with better outcomes and richer learning. The file size and detail level evidence this principle—more systematic design = better results."

These experiences collectively shaped my understanding of service design. I moved from viewing service as "what I deliver" to viewing it as "the systematic framework that ensures consistent value delivery." The progression from individual app builds to template creation to partnership system design represents this evolution.
