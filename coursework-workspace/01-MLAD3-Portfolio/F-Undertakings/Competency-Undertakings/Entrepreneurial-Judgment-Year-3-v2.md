## Competency

**What is the name and definition of this competency?**

Entrepreneurial Judgment: The ability to make strategic decisions under uncertainty, validate assumptions before significant investment, allocate resources to highest-impact opportunities based on evidence rather than speculation, and balance ambition with realistic assessment. This encompasses evidence-based decision-making, opportunity cost analysis, strategic patience, and honest self-assessment.

**How is this competency relevant to my situation and SMART Objectives?**

Entrepreneurial judgment is the meta-competency that informs all strategic decisions. The progression from SMART Objective 6 original target (100 partnerships by April 2026) to the updated stretch goal (500 partnerships by November 2026) requires judgment: validate the 100-partner model before committing to 500.

This competency also connects to target adjustments. The cannabis-free target adjustment (100% → 80%) and focused hours adjustment (70 → 50) both demonstrate entrepreneurial judgment: recognising that perfect targets aren't achievable during rapid scaling, adjusting to realistic targets that maintain ambition while acknowledging reality.

## Key Learning

One significant learning moment came from the evidence-based scaling approach. The 100-partnership target isn't just a goal—it's a validation point. Achieving it provides data: pricing validated, unit economics positive, operational capacity proven, team development started. The insight was: "Strategic decisions require evidence validation, not speculative projections. Validating 100-partner model before 500-partners prevents premature scaling." This evidence-based approach directly contrasts with Year 2 mistake.

Another key learning came from target adjustments (November 2025 - January 2026). Time-tracking app data revealed ~50% actual compliance vs 100% target. I could continue claiming 100% or adjust to honest 80% target. I chose honesty. The insight was: "Entrepreneurial judgment requires honest self-assessment. Lying to yourself destroys judgment capability. Data > ego."

A third insight emerged from opportunity cost analysis. In October-December 2025, I had multiple potential opportunities: new products, market expansion, partnership focus. I evaluated using analysis framework: revenue potential, execution complexity, time to market, strategic fit. Partnership focus scored highest. I declined others and focused exclusively. Result: progress toward 100-partnership target. The insight: "Entrepreneurial judgment = saying no to good opportunities to focus on great opportunities."

A fourth learning moment came from Construction Deal Collapse (LbD-06). The deal fell through after multiple conversations. Initially disappointed, I recognised that deal fallout is normal, not personal. The insight: "Entrepreneurial judgment includes emotional resilience. Deals fall through for reasons beyond your control. Rejection isn't about competence—it's about fit, timing, circumstances."

These experiences collectively shaped my understanding of entrepreneurial judgment. I moved from viewing decisions as binary (right/wrong) to viewing them as probabilistic (what's likelihood this will work, based on what evidence?). The progression from Year 2 (speculative execution) to Year 3 (evidence-based validation) represents significant development.

## Progression through Enquiry

Enquiry-based learning was essential for developing entrepreneurial judgment capability. LfO research undertakings provided frameworks for strategic decision-making.

Key frameworks studied:

**Lean Startup Methodology (Eric Ries):** Build-Measure-Learn cycle. Minimum Viable Product → measure outcomes → learn → iterate. Validated learning > vanity metrics. Fail fast, fail cheap, learn faster. Applied to partnership scaling: validate 100-partner model before 500-partner commitment.

**First Principles Thinking:** Break problems to fundamental truths, build up from there, rather than reasoning by analogy. Applied to capacity planning: what is sustainable capacity? Not "what do others do?" but "what does data show about my actual capacity?" Answer: 50 hours sustainable.

**Opportunity Cost Analysis:** Every decision has opportunity cost—resources are finite. Best opportunity is good in absolute terms AND better than alternatives. Applied to strategic focus decision.

**Validated Learning:** Learning only "validated" when tested through empirical experimentation. Opinions and hypotheses are not evidence. LfO research tested assumptions with real data. 100-partnership target validates business model with actual data.

**Strategic Patience:** Successful companies validate before scale; failed companies scale before validation. Strategic patience to validate 100-partner model before 500-partner commitment requires resisting growth pressure.

How this influenced my professional values and practices:

Strategic decision-making research reinforced commitment to evidence-based processes. Old approach: set ambitious targets, figure out execution. New approach: validate at small scale, commit to scale. 100-partnership target is validation point; 500-partnership target is scaling commitment.

I also developed appreciation for systematic decision-making frameworks. Entrepreneurial judgment isn't about gut instinct—it's about following processes that increase probability of good outcomes.

By integrating this knowledge, I've made better strategic decisions. Evidence-based scaling approach prevents repeating Year 2 mistake. Honest target adjustments demonstrate realistic self-assessment. Strategic focus enables progress toward 100-partnership target.

However, entrepreneurial judgment development is ongoing. True test: will judgment quality hold as challenges increase in complexity? 500-partner scaling will bring new decisions requiring good judgment.
