## ğŸ“ LfO UNDERTAKING STRUCTURE

**Word Count:** ~500-600 words each

### Section 1: Activity (50-100 words)
- **What:** Specific learning activity
- **When:** Date and duration
- **Why:** Connection to your learning journey
- **Format:** Type of source (book, event, course, etc.)

### Section 2: Critical Evaluation (150-200 words)
- **What insights did you gain?**
- **What challenged your assumptions?**
- **What surprised you?**
- **How did it change your thinking?**

### Section 3: Synthesis (100-150 words)
- **How does this connect to other sources/topics?**
- **How does it align or conflict with prior learning?**
- **Integration with theory/frameworks**

### Section 4: Application (100-150 words)
- **How have you applied this learning?**
- **Specific examples from your practice**
- **Impact on your ventures/projects/team**

### Section 5: Validity Evaluation (50-100 words)
- **Source credibility assessment**
- **Strengths and limitations**
- **Relevance to your context**

## ğŸ’¡ OUTSTANDING QUALITY INDICATORS

For 90-100% (Outstanding) on LfO Undertakings:

**Source Quality:**
- [ ] High-quality, credible sources
- [ ] Diverse source types (not just 6 books)
- [ ] Recent sources where relevant
- [ ] Specific sources identified (author, title, date)

**Critical Evaluation:**
- [ ] Genuine insights (not obvious observations)
- [ ] Challenged assumptions (not just confirmation)
- [ ] Surprising discoveries
- [ ] Changed thinking or behavior

**Synthesis:**
- [ ] Connections to other learning sources
- [ ] Integration with theories or frameworks
- [ ] Conflicts or contradictions addressed
- [ ] Broader context considered

- [ ] Specific examples of application (not "I will use this")
- [ ] Measurable impact on ventures/projects
- [ ] Connection to SMART Objectives
- [ ] Clear before/after comparison

- [ ] Honest source critique
- [ ] Limitations acknowledged
- [ ] Relevance to your context evaluated
- [ ] Bias or perspective considered
