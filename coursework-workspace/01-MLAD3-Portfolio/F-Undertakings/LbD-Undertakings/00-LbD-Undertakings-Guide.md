## ğŸ“‹ WHAT ARE LbD UNDERTAKINGS?

**LbD = Learning by Doing**

These document learning from practical activities including:
- Project work (Team Apollo, client projects, internal ventures)
- Business development activities (partnership outreach, sales)
- Product development (app development, system building)
- Team leadership activities (delegation, facilitation)
- Experiments and pilots (testing new approaches)
- Challenges or failures (learning from what went wrong)

---

## ğŸ“ LbD UNDERTAKING STRUCTURE

**Word Count:** ~500-600 words each

### Section 1: Activity (50-100 words)
- **What:** Specific practical activity or project
- **When:** Date and duration
- **Why:** Connection to your learning journey
- **Context:** Your role and environment

### Section 2: Critical Evaluation (150-200 words)
- **What insights did you gain from doing this?**
- **What challenged your assumptions?**
- **What surprised you about the experience?**
- **How did it change your approach?**

### Section 3: Synthesis (100-150 words)
- **How does this connect to other learning experiences?**
- **How does it align or conflict with prior knowledge?**
- **Integration with theories or frameworks**

### Section 4: Application (100-150 words)
- **How have you applied this learning since?**
- **Specific examples of changed behavior/decisions**
- **Impact on subsequent activities/projects**

### Section 5: Validity Evaluation (50-100 words)
- **Assessment of the learning experience itself
- **What was valuable about this approach?**
- **Limitations of what you learned**
- **Relevance to future practice**

## ğŸ’¡ OUTSTANDING QUALITY INDICATORS

For 90-100% (Outstanding) on LbD Undertakings:

**Activity Quality:**
- [ ] Specific, concrete activities (not vague "I worked on X")
- [ ] Challenging activities (not routine tasks)
- [ ] Clear outcomes (success, failure, or mixed)
- [ ] Your role clearly defined

**Critical Evaluation:**
- [ ] Genuine insights from the experience
- [ ] Honest assessment of what went wrong
- [ ] Surprising discoveries (not just confirmation)
- [ ] Changed approach or behavior as result

**Synthesis:**
- [ ] Connections to other experiences
- [ ] Integration with learning from LfO sources
- [ ] Theoretical frameworks applied
- [ ] Patterns identified across experiences

**Application:**
- [ ] Specific changes in behavior/decisions
- [ ] Measurable improvements in subsequent activities
- [ ] Connection to SMART Objective progress
- [ ] Clear before/after comparison

**Validity Assessment:**
- [ ] Honest evaluation of the learning value
- [ ] Limitations acknowledged (what you still don't know)
- [ ] Relevance to future practice considered
- [ ] Alternative approaches considered

## ğŸ”— DIFFERENCES: LfO vs LbD

| Aspect | LfO (Learning from Others) | LbD (Learning by Doing) |
|--------|---------------------------|------------------------|
| **Source** | External (books, courses, mentors) | Internal (projects, activities, experiments) |
| **Focus** | What others taught you | What you learned from experience |
| **Validity** | Source credibility | Experience quality |
| **Application** | How you applied external learning | How you applied lessons from doing |
