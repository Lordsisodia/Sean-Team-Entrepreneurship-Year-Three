## 🔄 TEAM REVIEW 1: End of October 2025

### Feedback Received:

**From whom:** Sam, Leo, David, Harry (ongoing 1-on-1 and 1-on-2 feedback sessions throughout October 2025, including while in Bali)

**On what objectives/areas:**
- Delegation and management style (Sam)
- Communication and prioritization (Leo)
- Partnership strategy and product design (David)
- Team dynamics and mentoring approach (Harry)

**What was said:**

**Sam's Feedback:**
- *Current delegation style:* "Very loose and informal. We catch up to see what needs doing, allocate tasks, then just do them. Talk only if we need help."
- *Sam's request:* "Ask me for help more often so I'm kept in the loop. This would allow you to provide insight into how to do things more efficiently."
- *Underlying issue:* Sam needs to use AI more effectively to delegate and handle tasks independently
- *On objectives:* Sam suggested reviewing all 6 objectives together and providing feedback (acknowledged as likely discussion point)

**Leo's Feedback:**
- *On bronze/silver/gold tier proposal:* "Great idea. More than 40% got implemented for my project."
- *On communication style:* "Good. We talk a lot about business." Main issue: "You're too busy. You don't have enough time to talk to people because you're working so hard."
- *On technical vs. strategic:* After 4-5 hours talking with Leo's agency developer, it became clear "these devs are quite behind on new AI frameworks and technical curves." I ended up giving technical advice; Leo and I mostly discuss industry potential.
- *On time management:* Leo knows my time management is "out the window" because I focus so intensely on single tasks that I spend all my time on them
- *On team adoption:* No direct suggestions, but using first principles thinking: (1) Need most efficient off-boarded intelligence, (2) Cheapest/most efficient option is AI, (3) Must train AI to handle specific tasks or systemize for repeatable execution

**David's Feedback:**
- *On product design:* "Busy agency owners won't complete technical setup themselves."
- *My agreement:* "100% agree. That's why we made setup very easy - can be completed over time, not all at once. Log in, create app, connect phone number. Agent even guides them on their own time."
- *Product response:* We pushed product update forward to address this specific feedback
- *On partnership progress:* "Poor." We've gone through 12-15 partners, have 3 active ones, but closed others because we're specifically targeting e-commerce clients
- *On pricing:* Everyone (first principles to AI, people around me) says charge retainer. Current model: Upfront setup fee (£1,000 → target £2,000) + monthly retainer (£50-£200/month for 24/7 on-call dev services, worth £1,000-£2,000/month)
- *On outreach strategy:* David suggested "find specific groups and communities where agency owners hang out and message them directly there"

**Harry's Feedback:**
- *On NHS outreach:* Harry mentioned this, but I don't recall doing NHS outreach - may have been miscommunication or different project
- *On micromanagement:* Harry says I only micromanage when training/teaching, which I believe is the best way to get people up to speed quickly
- *Example:* Recently trained "my boy" on Claude Code for 4 hours - went from zero to hero on Supabase, Vercel, building full-stack apps. He now knows everything needed to do it indefinitely and continue self-learning
- *On team dynamics:* Harry says we need to be more "team-like" rather than just independents working toward the same goal
- *On mentoring style:* No one has said anything bad about it. Team always achieves what they need to do. The intensive training approach works.
- *On emotional intelligence:* "I love feedback, especially negative feedback because it gives me more to learn from."
- *On people leadership improvement:* "Be less busy and have less on your mind. Offload intelligence and compute so it can be handled elsewhere. Only you can lay it out and plan it - need things working in background."

**Themes identified:**
- **Theme 1: Workload Management** - Everyone (Sam, Leo, Harry) agreed: "You're too busy." Need to offload intelligence to AI/systems
- **Theme 2: Delegation vs. Training Trade-off** - Sam wants more guidance/loop-in; Harry acknowledges my intensive training style works but creates busyness
- **Theme 3: Strategic Focus** - David confirmed need to focus on e-commerce clients; Leo confirmed AI expertise is valuable differentiator
- **Theme 4: Communication Throughput** - Leo says communication is good but volume is the constraint (not enough time for everyone)

---

### Your Response:

**Initial reaction:**
I appreciated the feedback because it confirmed patterns I was observing but hadn't fully articulated. The consistent message across all four people was clear: **I'm doing the right work, but I'm the bottleneck.** The "too busy" refrain from Sam, Leo, and Harry validated that my intensive focus approach (while effective for skill transfer) creates capacity constraints. I didn't feel defensive—I felt relieved that the solution wasn't to work differently, but to offload more.

**Your perspective:**
I agree with all the feedback received. Sam's request for more loop-in makes sense—I currently default to "autonomy unless requested" when "guided autonomy with check-ins" would be more effective. Leo's observation about my time management being "out the window" due to hyperfocus is accurate—I've known this about myself for years but seeing it impact my team creates new urgency to solve it. David's feedback on product design confirmed what I already suspected but was resisting for simplicity. Harry's point about being "less busy" by offloading intelligence is exactly the direction I need to go.

The only point of confusion was Harry's mention of NHS outreach, which I don't recall doing—this may have been a miscommunication or referring to a different project context.

**Your rationale:**
What my team doesn't fully see is that my "busyness" comes from a deliberate choice: I prioritize depth of execution over breadth of coordination. The 4-hour intensive training session I did for "my boy" transferred months of learning in a single session. Leo's agency developer conversation (4-5 hours) revealed months of knowledge gaps. Sam's loose delegation style works because I've invested heavily in upfront capability transfer.

However, this approach doesn't scale. As Leo pointed out using first principles: I need "most efficient off-boarded intelligence possible" which means AI and systems, not more intensive 1-on-1 training. The feedback confirmed I'm at an inflection point: my high-touch approach has worked for building initial capability, but now I need to transition to high-system approach for scaling.

---

### Actions Taken:

**What changed:**
1. **Product Design (David's feedback):** Pushed product update forward to make setup easier - agencies can complete incrementally over time, not all at once
2. **Partnership Strategy (David's feedback):** Focused on e-commerce clients specifically, closed off other verticals to concentrate efforts
3. **Pricing Model (David's + general feedback):** Shifted toward retainer model - upfront setup fee (£1,000 → £2,000 target) + monthly retainer (£50-£200/month for 24/7 on-call services)
4. **Outreach Strategy (David's feedback):** Started identifying specific communities/groups where e-commerce agency owners congregate for direct messaging
5. **Strategic Direction (Leo's feedback):** Recognized that AI/systems must handle repeatable tasks - "only I can lay it out and plan it" so need to build background systems
6. **Delegation Approach (Sam's feedback):** Need to move from "autonomy unless requested" to "guided autonomy with regular check-ins" - Sam explicitly asked for more loop-in and insight sharing

**Timeline:**
- Product and pricing changes: Implemented November-December 2025
- Delegation approach improvements: Ongoing (need to implement more structured check-ins)
- AI/systematization: Recognized as priority, need implementation

**Measurement:**
- **Sam's independence:** Can Sam handle tasks without asking for help? (Current: mixed - sometimes needs guidance)
- **Leo's time perception:** Does Leo feel I'm more available? (Need to measure communication frequency)
- **David's partnership progress:** Are we acquiring e-commerce agencies more effectively? (3 active partners targeting e-commerce)
- **Harry's "team-like" metric:** Does the team feel more cohesive or still like independents? (Need to assess)
- **Busyness reduction:** Am I spending less time in execution and more in systematization? (Need to track)

---

### Reflection:

**What you learned:**
About myself: My "intensive training" style (4-hour zero-to-hero sessions) creates strong results but creates dependency. I'm the bottleneck because I hoard context and haven't systematized my knowledge. The feedback confirmed that AI/systematization is the only way to scale my expertise.

About your team: Sam wants more guidance, not less. Leo values strategic discussion over technical detail. David focuses on practical business fundamentals (ease of use, cash flow). Harry sees the team dynamic clearly ("independents working toward same goal" vs. "team-like").

About your objectives: Your partnership progress is "poor" not because of lack of effort, but because of focus issues. Having 12-15 partners in evaluation but only 3 active shows you're qualifying well but not committing. This is actually good discipline.

About the feedback process: Receiving consistent feedback from multiple sources (Sam, Leo, David, Harry) over time rather than one session provided better data points. The patterns that emerged across all four people were clearer than any single piece of feedback.

**What surprised you:**
Leo's agency developer being "quite behind on new AI frameworks" was surprising - I expected agency developers to be more cutting-edge. This validated that my AI expertise is a genuine differentiator, not just perception. The fact that more than 40% of my tier proposal got implemented (Leo said "great idea") showed that my ideas are sound - execution is the constraint, not strategy.

**What you'd do differently:**
In future feedback sessions: I'll proactively seek feedback from multiple team members over time rather than treating it as a one-time event. The 1-on-1 and 1-on-2 format worked well for gathering honest input.

For delegation: I'll implement more structured check-ins with Sam rather than waiting for him to ask. His explicit request ("ask me for help more often") should have been a signal to change my default approach.

For systematization: I'll prioritize building AI agents and documentation over doing more intensive training. The "zero to hero" approach works for initial skill transfer but doesn't scale - need to capture that knowledge in systems.
