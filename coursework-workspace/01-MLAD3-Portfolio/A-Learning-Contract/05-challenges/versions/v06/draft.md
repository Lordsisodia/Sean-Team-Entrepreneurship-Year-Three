Year 2 revealed six challenges that limit my effectiveness and team growth. Each challenge has specific impacts, root causes, and informs my Year 3 competency development.

## Challenge 1: Delegation Resistance

In September 2024, delegating partnership outreach to Sam lasted 3 days before I intervened. Watching his email template mistakes triggered immediate anxiety about quality. By Day 4, I reclaimed the task, worked until 3 AM fixing his approach, and delayed a client project by two days.

**Root Cause:** INTJ analytical precision combined with high "Control" motivation creates perfectionism disguised as quality standards (Briggs Myers et al., 1998). I unconsciously equate "doing it myself" with excellence.

**Impact on Me:** Creates bottlenecks limiting my focus on high-value strategic work. I can only scale to what I personally execute, which caps venture growth.

**Impact on Others:** Sam's suggestion frequency dropped 80% from 5 per week to 1 per week. During our October 3rd feedback session, David told me: "We don't bother proposing ideas because you'll just redo them anyway." I created dependency, not empowerment.

**Attempted Solutions:** I tried using a "24-hour rule"—waiting 24 hours before intervening on delegated tasks. This failed because I still checked work constantly and found excuses to intervene. I'm now exploring pre-work collaboration frameworks that involve team members in strategy creation rather than post-hoc correction.

**Competency Link:** This challenge directly informs Year 3 development of **Team-Development: Mentoring Others** competency (UWE, 2024).

## Challenge 2: App Development Priority Management

I spent 6 weeks building an internal time management app while partnership apps remain incomplete. The internal app felt satisfying—I could optimize every feature. Meanwhile, Sam couldn't effectively onboard partners because the infrastructure didn't exist.

**Root Cause:** Perfectionism prioritized over market testing. When Pranav asked why we weren't prioritizing client revenue, I said: "We need to build this right the first time." He replied: "We need good enough now, not perfect eventually."

**Impact on Me:** Delays core business scaling and revenue generation. Every week on internal tools is a week not on revenue-generating work. Perfectionism prevents market learning.

**Impact on Others:** Sam can't close partnerships without onboarding infrastructure. Team morale suffers when I prioritize what interests me over what sustains us collectively. Partners cannot be onboarded, creating reputation damage.

**Attempted Solutions:** I implemented a "revenue-first" rule in October—no internal tooling until client-facing systems ship. This lasted two weeks before I rationalized building an "essential" automation. The rule failed because I defined "client-facing" narrowly to justify internal work.

**Competency Link:** Informs **Venture: Prioritisation & Strategic Focus** competency development (UWE, 2024).

## Challenge 3: Revenue Generation Delay

I focus on systems optimization while avoiding revenue pursuit, dismissing smaller opportunities because "couple grand a month doesn't interest me." When a £5,000/month retainer emerged, I passed as "too small" relative to my "couple million per month" vision.

**Root Cause:** Ambition has become avoidance. I avoid smaller revenue opportunities that would fund growth while waiting for perfect opportunities. My Development motivation (UWE, 2024) drives future-focus at expense of present sustainability.

**Impact on Me:** Creates financial sustainability risks. Can't leap from £0 to £1M/month without intermediate steps. Missing market timing during rapid AI industry changes.

**Impact on Others:** Team confidence suffers when I dismiss revenue opportunities. Sam sees viable partnerships I reject as "too small," undermining his motivation. Every rejected £5,000/month opportunity is £60,000/year lost for team financial security.

**Attempted Solutions:** I set a minimum revenue target of £10,000/month for any opportunity consideration. This created filter failure: I still rejected opportunities because I mentally discounted their potential. The rule didn't address the root issue—reluctance to do sales work.

**Competency Link:** Informs **Venture: Commercial Awareness** competency development (UWE, 2024).

## Challenge 4: Team Leadership Implementation

I provide technical direction without ensuring adoption, taking attitude that "it's not really my fault if they don't implement." When Harry questioned my AI recommendation, I explained why I was correct rather than understanding his concerns. Result: he didn't adopt the solution.

**Root Cause:** INTJ temperament values logical analysis over influence (Briggs Myers et al., 1998). I see leadership as providing good answers rather than ensuring adoption. Being right without being influential is leadership failure.

**Impact on Me:** Limits effectiveness as a leader. Partnership framework had 40% adoption because I hadn't involved team in creation. My expertise creates zero value if no one adopts it.

**Impact on Others:** Reduces team capability development. When I provide answers without ensuring understanding, team can't develop judgment. Team idea generation dropped 60% in Q3 after I shut down suggestions with technical arguments.

**Attempted Solutions:** I started asking "How can I explain this better?" which failed because it assumed the problem was communication, not influence. I'm now learning that influence requires trust built before technical conversations, not during them.

**Competency Link:** Informs **Team-Development: Communication & Influence** competency (UWE, 2024).

## Challenge 5: Partnership Acquisition Experience

I currently have zero agency partnerships despite 1,000 partnership goal in 9 months. In four conversations, I generated genuine interest then failed to ask for commitment. Drop-off rate: 75%. Sam said: "You create beautiful frameworks but avoid asking for commitment."

**Root Cause:** Comfortable with technical discussions but avoid sales conversations. Current pace ~10/month achieved vs. ~23/month needed—gap isn't effort but capability I don't have.

**Impact on Me:** Threatens exit strategy. "Couple million per month" vision depends entirely on partnerships. Need rapid skill development in sales, outreach, closing.

**Impact on Others:** Team's financial future depends on partnership success. Sam does excellent outreach generating interest that I then fail to convert. This wastes his effort and damages motivation. Every unclosed partnership represents lost revenue for team operations.

**Attempted Solutions:** I prepared closing scripts but couldn't use them authentically. I tried pushing harder for commitment which made conversations feel transactional. The real issue: I need to learn sales as skill, not hack it with scripts or force.

**Competency Link:** Informs **Venture: Sales & Partnership Development** competency (UWE, 2024).

## Challenge 6: Time Management Across Projects

Managing multiple priorities without systematic prioritization. In December 2024, I switched tasks 47 times weekly, delivered Theo's project at 3 AM, missed 3 team sessions, and arrived late to Harry's planning meeting.

**Root Cause:** "Active Changer" learning style craves novelty causing task-switching. High "Development" motivation makes me say "yes" to everything (UWE, 2024). Mid-range Conscientiousness means I lack systematic prioritisation.

**Impact on Me:** Chronic crisis mode creates exhaustion. Always busy but rarely productive on highest-impact tasks. Task-switching prevents deep strategic work. This isn't sustainable.

**Impact on Others:** My unpredictability creates team anxiety. Harry stated: "I can't plan around you when you're constantly in crisis mode." Leo's client satisfaction dropped from 9.2 to 7.1/10 when delayed. My chaos damages team credibility.

**Attempted Solutions:** I tried time-blocking apps but abandoned them when imperfect. I attempted "three priorities only" rule but broke it within days. The issue: I prioritize what feels urgent moment-to-moment rather than what's strategically important. Need systematic frameworks, not willpower.

**Competency Link:** Informs **Self-Management: Time & Priority Management** competency (UWE, 2024).

---

## Integrated Root Cause Pattern

All six challenges stem from over-reliance on autonomous execution at expense of collaborative intelligence and strategic awareness. INTJ strategic thinking creates false confidence that "I can figure this out myself" (Briggs Myers et al., 1998), when sustainable scaling requires systematic processes, diverse input, and market intelligence. These challenges directly inform Year 3 competency development across Self-Management, Team-Development, and Venture areas.
