## Challenge 1: Delegation Resistance and Control Attachment

In September 2024, delegating partnership outreach to Sam lasted 3 days before I intervened. Watching his email template mistakes triggered immediate anxiety about quality. By Day 4, I reclaimed the task, worked until 3 AM fixing his approach, and delayed a client project by two days. My delegation resistance stems from control needs, not efficiency.

**Root Cause:** INTJ analytical precision combined with high "Control" motivation creates perfectionism disguised as quality standards. I unconsciously equate "doing it myself" with excellence. The Strengths Assessment's problem-solving strength becomes a weakness when I won't let others learn through trial.

**Impact:** Sam's suggestion frequency dropped 80% from 5 per week to 1 per week after September. He stopped initiating improvements entirely. During our October 3rd feedback session, David told me, "We don't bother proposing ideas because you'll just redo them anyway." I created dependency, not empowerment. My Growth value claims development, but my behaviour contradicts this—undermining integrity and trust.

**Year 2 Evolution:** In Year 2, I framed this as "team capability issues." Now I recognise it's my control attachment preventing team growth.

## Challenge 2: Time Fragmentation and Prioritisation Failure

Last month (December 2024), three client deadlines and four internal projects resulted in attempting everything, switching tasks 47 times weekly. I delivered Theo's project at 3 AM, missed 3 team entrepreneurship sessions, and arrived late to Harry's January 8th planning meeting, forcing a reschedule. This isn't "working hard"—it's chaotic prioritisation.

**Root Cause:** My "Active Changer" learning style craves novelty, causing rapid task-switching. High "Development" motivation makes me say "yes" to everything. Mid-range Conscientiousness means I lack systematic prioritisation. I work on what feels urgent, not what's strategically important.

**Impact:** Leo's project was delayed 4 days because I couldn't resist checking "quick things" elsewhere. His client satisfaction score dropped from 9.2 to 7.1/10. During our January 10th feedback session, Harry stated, "I can't plan around you when you're constantly in crisis mode." My unpredictability creates team anxiety. My Responsibility value claims clear guidelines, but fragmented schedules model the opposite behaviour—damaging credibility.

**Year 2 Evolution:** Year 2 assessments praised my "work ethic." Now I see this as undisciplined prioritisation masquerading as dedication.

## Challenge 3: Emotional Intelligence Gap in Team Dynamics

During September 2024's strategic planning session, David suggested pivoting outreach. My immediate reaction was defensive dismissal: "That won't work based on current data." The room went silent. During our private feedback session on September 17th, Sam told me, "We stopped sharing ideas because you shoot them down immediately." Three team members withdrew from strategic discussions that month.

**Root Cause:** INTJ temperament values logical analysis over emotional process. High Assertiveness plus mid-range Aggressive tendencies means I dominate discussions without sensing withdrawal. Low Extraversion means I miss subtle cues. I prioritise being "right" over being effective.

**Impact:** David stopped contributing entirely. During our October 5th one-to-one, Leo told me, "It's not worth the energy to debate you." Team idea generation dropped 60% in Q3. I silenced diverse perspectives, reducing innovation. My Impact value claims driving meaningful outcomes, but suppressing input undermines collective intelligence—violating collaborative leadership ethics.

**Year 2 Evolution:** Year 2 feedback called me "decisive." I now recognise this as shutting down dialogue under the guise of efficiency.

## Challenge 4: Commercial Awareness Blind Spot

Despite running multiple businesses, I missed Q3 2024's market shift towards AI automation. Competitors launched tools I should have built, costing £3,000 revenue. During our November 12th strategy review, Matt asked, "Why didn't we see this trend?" The information was available—I wasn't looking. This represents systematic commercial blindness.

**Root Cause:** The Strengths Assessment explicitly flags commercial awareness as underdeveloped. My "Reflective Realist" learning needs structured reflective learning, yet I allocate zero time for market analysis. High Openness focuses on future possibilities over current market realities. I prioritise technical innovation over commercial positioning.

**Impact:** Harry's sales team couldn't answer client positioning questions. Our conversion rate dropped 15% in October. During our November 8th feedback session, Jaymie noted, "We're losing deals because we can't articulate market differentiation." Technical focus without commercial context limits revenue capacity. My Autonomy value drives independence, but commercial ignorance undermines strategic autonomy.

**Year 2 Evolution:** Year 2 business reviews focused on technical features. Now I recognise commercial blindness as my biggest strategic vulnerability.

## Integrated Root Cause Pattern

All four challenges stem from over-reliance on autonomous execution at the expense of collaborative intelligence and strategic awareness. INTJ strategic thinking creates false confidence that "I can figure this out myself," when sustainable scaling requires systematic processes, diverse input, and market intelligence. These challenges directly inform Year 3 competency development in Collaboration, Strategic Thinking, Self-Awareness, and Business Analysis.
