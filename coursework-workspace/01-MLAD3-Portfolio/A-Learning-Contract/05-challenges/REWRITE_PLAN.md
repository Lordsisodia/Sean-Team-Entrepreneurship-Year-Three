## DETAILED REWRITE STRUCTURE

### Challenge 1: Delegation Resistance (~120 words)

**Opening Statement:**
> "My INTJ preference for independent work, combined with high Control motivation, creates a systematic delegation resistance that slows team capacity building."

**Specific Example (Team Apollo):**
- **When:** September 2024
- **Who:** Sam (partnership lead)
- **What:** Delegated partnership outreach, lasted 3 days before stepping back in
- **Feelings:** Anxious watching him make mistakes, frustrated with approach
- **Outcome:** Sam disengaged, I was bottleneck, partnership progress stalled

**Root Cause Connection:**
> "I tell myself 'I'll spend more time explaining than doing it myself,' but this rationalization masks my discomfort with losing control. When Sam's outreach approach differed from mine, I felt anxious about potential mistakes—revealing that my perfectionism prevents others from learning through doing."

**Impact:**
> "This pattern creates bottlenecks and prevents team skill development. When I stepped back in after 3 days, Sam's engagement dropped, and we lost momentum on partnership conversations that had taken weeks to establish."

### Challenge 2: Team Adoption Gap (~120 words)

**Opening Statement:**
> "My 40% team adoption rate for suggestions and tools reveals that my assertive communication style, without sufficient collaborative sensitivity, creates resistance rather than alignment."

**Specific Example (Team Apollo):**
- **When:** October 2024 (client onboarding)
- **Who:** Pranav + Sam
- **What:** Built PDR automation system, only I understood it
- **Feelings:** Frustrated they couldn't see efficiency, defensive when questioned
- **Outcome:** 3 days lost when I was busy, system became bottleneck

**Root Cause Connection:**
> "My mid-range aggressive tendencies mean I prioritize being 'right' over being effective. When Pranav questioned whether the AI-first approach was necessary, I pushed ahead anyway instead of taking time to build buy-in. The system I built was technically sophisticated but practically inaccessible—a pattern where I optimize for my standards rather than team usability."

**Impact:**
> "When the automation system needed maintenance during client onboarding, neither Sam nor Pranav could help. We lost 3 days of progress, and clients received delayed responses. This taught me that technical leadership without stakeholder alignment creates technical debt, not competitive advantage."

### Challenge 3: Priority Management Struggle (~100 words)

**Opening Statement:**
> "My stated value of 'continuous learning' conflicts with my practice of skipping learning blocks when partnership outreach feels urgent—revealing a values-practice gap that reduces my effectiveness."

**Specific Example (Team Apollo):**
- **When:** September 2024
- **Who:** Sam (witnessed the pattern)
- **What:** Missed 4 of 8 learning sessions for outreach
- **Feelings:** Guilty, rushed through outreach without presence
- **Outcome:** Reduced effectiveness in both areas

**Root Cause Connection:**
> "My mid-range conscientiousness means I don't maintain systematic prioritization. I work on what feels urgent rather than what's strategically important, reacting to immediate pressures instead of protecting time for long-term development. When Sam asked why I was skipping learning sessions, I couldn't articulate a rationale—I just felt pulled toward outreach."

**Impact:**
> "This values-practice gap caused me guilt and reduced effectiveness. I was rushing through outreach without being fully present, missing 4 learning opportunities that would have developed capabilities for the next quarter. I'm still learning where to draw the line between immediate business needs and long-term development."
