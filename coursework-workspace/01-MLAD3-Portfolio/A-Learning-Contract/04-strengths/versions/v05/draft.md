My Year 2 psychometric assessments revealed six strengths that define my self-management approach. Each has a shadow side I'm learning to balance.

## Values-Based Strengths

**Integrity-Based Transparency**

Transparency became my strength through failure. On September 12, 2024, I spent two weeks building an automation system in secret, excited to surprise Sam. When I revealed it, he felt excluded and questioned my judgment. I felt ashamed. During our one-to-one on September 16, Sam said: "When you hide your work, I don't know if you're actually productive or just avoiding collaboration." That feedback shifted my behaviour. Now I share my thinking in real-time through Slack. However, I over-explain. During October 3 team standup, David interrupted: "You're explaining the obvious again—we get it." Transparency isn't about volume—it's about relevance.

**Ethical Rigor**

My ethical commitment was forged when Harry suggested grey-hat automation tactics on October 8, 2024. I refused, feeling physically sick at the thought of violating platform terms. I value reputation over quick wins. But this becomes self-righteousness. When Sam explored a cannabis industry partnership on November 14, I dismissed it arrogantly as "not aligned with our values." Leo pulled me aside after the meeting: "You're being judgmental, not principled. You can disagree without being superior." That stung—ethical leadership requires nuance, not absolutism.

## Behavioural Strengths

**Systematic Thinking**

Systematic thinking emerged from frustration. On August 22, 2024, I spent two weeks manually researching crypto prospects, exhausted and missing crucial information. I felt foolish. That sparked my obsession with efficient systems, leading to the 75-step PDR automation. But this morphs into perfectionism avoidance. When Sam asked on November 28 why I was still optimizing the Partnership Framework instead of doing outreach, I felt defensive—I was hiding in planning to avoid rejection. The framework launched January 6 with only 40% team adoption because I hadn't involved them in creation. Systematic thinking serves me only when balanced with action.

**Resilience Under Pressure**

Pressure reveals my capacity to focus. On November 19, 2024, a crypto client needed an MVP in 10 days. I worked 12-hour days, delivering on time and enabling them to secure £50,000 seed funding. Jaymie noted: "Your calm under pressure kept the team focused when we were panicking." Yet Emma observed during our retrospective on December 12: "You procrastinated on the partnership proposal until it became a crisis. You're addicted to urgency." She's right—I had three weeks to start that proposal but began November 15. This isn't resilience—it's poor time management masked as strength.

## Technical Strengths

**Technical Translation**

I translate complex technical concepts for non-technical audiences. When I created onboarding videos in January 2025, new client 90-day retention improved from 45% to 59% (measured March 31, 2025). But I over-simplify. During the AI architecture presentation on February 8, Harry said: "You're dumbing this down. I can't contribute if you don't trust me with the real complexity." I felt embarrassed. Translation requires balancing accessibility with depth, not assuming what others can understand.

**Automation Architecture**

I see automation opportunities others miss. When I automated intern tasks in December 2024, it cut manual workload from 30 hours weekly to 15 hours (measured through time-tracking January 2025). But this becomes paralysis. I spent three weeks in December refining the client intake process that worked adequately, delaying launch until January 8. During our sprint review on January 9, David said: "That's analysis paralysis—you're optimizing for perfection, not shipping." I realized I was afraid to ship something imperfect. Perfection is the enemy of good enough.

---

These six strengths define my leadership. Each emerged from specific team situations, adds value to Apollo, and becomes a weakness when overused. Year 3 will be about balancing these strengths with their shadow sides through self-awareness.
