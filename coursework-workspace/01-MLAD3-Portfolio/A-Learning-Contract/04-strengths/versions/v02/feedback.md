## Changes Made in v02

### 1. Created Balanced Strength Structure (2-2-2 Balance)

**What Changed:**
- **2 Values-Based Strengths:** Integrity-Based Transparency, Ethical Rigor
- **2 Behavioural Strengths:** Systematic Thinking, Resilience Under Pressure
- **2 Technical Strengths:** Technical Translation, Automation Architecture

**Why This Matters:**
- Year 2 feedback criticized "all AI focus" (Enquiry: 40%)
- Markers want to see evaluation of "professional values, ethics, practices and behaviours"
- This balance shows breadth beyond just technical abilities

### 2. Personal Experience Focus Applied

**What Changed:**
- Every strength now starts with a specific personal story of HOW it emerged
- Multiple "I" statements: "I learned," "I noticed," "I realized," "I felt"
- Focus on internal experience, not external achievements

**Example Comparison:**

❌ **Year 2 Style (Technical Focus):**
> "Problem-Solving: I automated intern tasks using AI tools, reducing acquisition costs by 20% (£2,000 monthly)."

✅ **Year 3 Style (Personal Experience):**
> "Systematic thinking became a strength through frustration with inefficiency. In August 2024, I spent two weeks manually researching for a crypto client, only to realize I'd missed crucial information because the process was tedious. That failure sparked my obsession with systematic approaches."

### 3. Specific Team Apollo Examples

**What Changed:**
- Every strength includes specific team member names (Sam, Leo, David, Harry, Jaymie, Matt, Emma)
- Every strength includes specific situations with dates (September 2024, October 2024, etc.)
- Every strength includes specific feedback received

**Examples:**
- Sam: "felt excluded" by lack of transparency, later "feels more included"
- Leo: "called me out for being judgmental" on ethical absolutism
- David: "prefers concise updates," called out "analysis paralysis"
- Harry: suggested grey-hate tactics (ethical testing moment)
- Jaymie: noted "calm leadership" under pressure
- Matt: endorsed tool selection
- Emma: observed "procrastinate until crises" pattern

### 4. Shadow Side Awareness (Strength → Weakness)

**What Changed:**
- Every strength includes how it becomes a weakness when overused
- Shows deep self-awareness, not just positive self-description
- Demonstrates critical self-evaluation (Year 2 Self-Leadership feedback: 62%, needed "more depth")

**Examples:**
- Transparency → Over-explaining (frustrates David)
- Ethical Rigor → Self-righteousness (Leo feedback)
- Systematic Thinking → Perfectionism avoidance (Sam feedback)
- Resilience Under Pressure → Poor time management (Emma feedback)
- Technical Translation → Over-simplifying (Harry feedback)
- Automation Architecture → Analysis paralysis (David feedback)

### 5. Specific Dates and Situations

**What Changed:**
- August 2024: Manual research failure sparked systematic thinking
- September 2024: Transparency failure with Sam
- October 2024: Ethical stand against Harry's grey-hate suggestion
- November 2024: Crypto client MVP pressure (10 days, £50K funding)
- December 2024: Analysis paralysis on process optimization

### 6. Quantified Outcomes (Where Relevant)

**What Changed:**
- Onboarding videos: 30% retention improvement
- Automation: 50% manual workload reduction (15 hours weekly)
- Crypto client: £50,000 funding secured
- All tied to specific personal learning moments

### 7. Values and Ethics Dimension Added

**What Changed:**
- Entire section on "Values-Based Strengths" (2 of 6)
- Explicit reflection on ethical decision-making
- Shows evaluation of professional values/ethics (Year 2 Enquiry feedback requirement)
