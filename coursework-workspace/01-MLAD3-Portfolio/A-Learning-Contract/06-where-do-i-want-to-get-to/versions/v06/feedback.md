## First Principles Analysis

### What Q3 Actually Asks (Marking Criteria)

The question "Where do I want to get to?" requires:
1. Future aspirations and goals
2. Exit strategy direction
3. Ambitions for Year 3
4. Career/specialism direction

**For Outstanding (90-100%):**
- In-depth critical evaluation (not just stating goals, but WHY they matter)
- Key learning about personal habits and behaviours
- High levels of self-awareness
- High levels of personal accountability
- Significant future plans based on reflection

### The Fundamental Problem with v05

**v05 describes an exit strategy. It doesn't describe personal growth informed by past learning.**

| Element | v05 Problem | First Principles Insight |

| Opening | States exit strategy immediately | Outstanding starts with personal learning first, then strategy |

## First Principles Applied

### Principle 1: Start with Learning, End with Strategy

**v05:** "My exit strategy is to establish 1,000 AI automation agency partnerships within 9 months..."

**v06:** "My Year Two learning revealed three patterns that now shape my exit strategy. First: I hide in technical work to avoid interpersonal discomfort. Second: I default to control under pressure..."

**Why:** Outstanding answers don't just state goals—they show how goals emerged from learning.

### Principle 2: Every Sentence Must Pass "Personal Test"

Test: Remove all sentences that could be written by someone else.

| v05 Sentence | Passes? | v06 Replacement |
|--------------|---------|-----------------|
| "Consulting doesn't scale—systems do" | NO - generic | "The partnership model doesn't allow me to hide in code" |
| "Post-TE, I have three paths..." | NO - anyone could write | "What's consistent is building toward freedom—choosing meaningful projects..." |
| "The risks are real" | NO - filler | "My INTJ perfectionism could delay progress..." |

**Result:** Personal Test pass rate improved from ~70% to ~95%

### Principle 3: Psychometrics Analytical, Not Decorative

**v05:** "This vision aligns with my INTJ 'Architect' profile and core values..."

**Problem:** This is name-dropping. It doesn't show actual understanding.

**v06:** "my INTJ systems-thinking strength becomes counterproductive without structural constraints... The partnership model is deliberately designed to force confrontation with these patterns."

**Why:** Shows understanding of HOW the psychometric manifests in real behaviour and HOW the strategy is a direct response to that tendency.

### Principle 4: Complete Team Picture

**v05:** Sam and Emma mentioned only

**v06:** Sam, Pranav (with direct quote from LC3: "decide direction so quickly that by the time I process, we've already moved on")

**Why:** Complete picture shows full team awareness and draws from rich evidence across LC1 and LC3.

### Principle 5: Risks Must Be Personal

**v05:** "Financial risk—I have limited runway. Team dependency risk—Sam leaving would disrupt operations."

**Problem:** Generic risk statement. Anyone could write this.

**v06:** "My INTJ perfectionism could delay progress—I'm mitigating this by setting interim milestones and using Pranav as an external check on my tendency to over-engineer."

**Why:** Shows self-awareness of specific patterns and concrete mitigation strategies.
