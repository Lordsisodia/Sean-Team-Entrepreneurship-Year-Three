# Section 08: Competencies I Will Develop

> Detailed breakdown of the six competencies selected for development during Q4, aligned to my learning objectives and development activities.

---

## Overview

I have selected six competencies for focused development during Q4 (January–March 2025): **2 Venture**, **2 Team**, **2 Personal**. Each links explicitly to Q4 development activities with measurable success indicators.

---

## 1. Strategic Leadership (Venture)

**Why:** Leading multi-stakeholder partnerships requires transitioning from founder to strategic leader. I must delegate operational responsibility to Sam whilst maintaining strategic coherence.

**Values:** Ethical delegation means empowering Sam with genuine decision-making authority whilst retaining accountability—transparent communication, support without micromanagement, tolerating short-term inefficiency for capability development.

**Activities:** Weekly delegation sessions; systems documentation; learning from TE alumni.

**Success:** Sam independently managing 50+ partnerships by March 2026.

---

## 2. Service Design & Delivery (Venture)

**Why:** I currently have zero agency partnerships yet target 1,000—this gap requires structured development. I need evidence-backed service models creating measurable mutual value.

**Values:** Co-creation ensures we design with, not for, users—actively seeking input, recognising power dynamics, iterating based on genuine feedback.

**Activities:** Pilots with 10 agencies; journey maps for experiences; studying successful partner programs.

**Success:** 100 validated partnerships by January 2026.

---

## 3. Professional Communication (Team)

**Why:** Working across contexts requires adapting communication. My 40% team adoption rate indicates I am good at information transfer but weak at influence.

**Values:** Ethical communication means active listening, acknowledging others' expertise, building consensus through genuine dialogue.

**Activities:** Communication skills training; applying "Start with Heart" principle; building influence through teaching TE workshops.

**Success:** Adoption rate increases from 40% to 80% by February 2026.

---

## 4. Financial Management (Team)

**Why:** Deploying team resources requires financial literacy beyond basic budgeting. I need cash flow understanding and resource optimisation to move from planning to execution.

**Values:** Ethical stewardship means transparent allocation decisions with clear rationale, balancing venture needs fairly, avoiding over-commitment to high-risk opportunities.

**Activities:** Building cash flow projections; creating real-time metric tracking; working with TE mentors.

**Success:** £5K+ monthly revenue by December 2025; financial models informing venture decisions.

---

## 5. Self-Management (Personal)

**Why:** September data revealed I spent 60% of time on delegable technical work and only 20% on partnership outreach. This drives systematic self-management.

**Values:** My time affects others—misallocation impacts team execution and partners' reliance. It requires honesty about avoidance patterns and willingness to prioritise strategic work.

**Activities:** Systematic time allocation via internal app; weekly priority reviews; using data to correct misalignment.

**Success:** 80% alignment by December 2025; technical work (60% → <30%); outreach (20% → >50%).

---

## 6. Entrepreneurial Judgment (Personal)

**Why:** My 6-week internal app mistake wasn't technical execution—it was strategic prioritisation. I must develop frameworks for questioning assumptions and making reasoned judgments under uncertainty.

**Values:** Examining positions of privilege, seeking diverse perspectives, acknowledging intuitive judgments may reflect bias, creating systematic checks to prevent ego-driven decisions.

**Activities:** Weekly strategic reviews; applying structured decision tools; learning from experienced entrepreneurs.

**Success:** Partnership/client apps completed by December 2025; decision frameworks applied to Q4 choices.

---

## Integration with Q4 Development

**Competency Connections:** Strategic Leadership + Professional Communication (delegation requires clear protocols), Service Design + Self-Management (scaling partnerships requires systematic time allocation), Financial Management + Entrepreneurial Judgment (resource allocation benefits from structured analysis).

**Timeline:** October-November builds foundations (pilots, dashboard, time-blocking), December-February scales what works (delegation accelerates, influence increases), March-April integrates all competencies.

All competencies connect directly to Section 07 activities and will be evidenced through portfolio outputs.
