# Section 08: Competencies I Will Develop

## Overview

Six competencies for Q4 (January–April 2026): **2 Venture**, **2 Team**, **2 Personal**. Each links to Q4 development activities with measurable success indicators.

---

## 1. Values Alignment (Personal: 4/10 → 8/10)

**Why:** When David suggested cutting corners on partner vetting, I felt uneasy but stayed silent. Sustainable partnerships require a values-based decision framework.

**Values:** Core principles (integrity, mutual value, long-term focus) with decision trees for ethical dilemmas.

**Activities:** Weekly values reflection; creating framework with TE mentor by late January; reviewing all partnership decisions against framework.

**Success:** 80% of partnership decisions values-consistent by March 2026 (measured through weekly self-audit journal, reviewed biweekly by TE mentor).

---

## 2. Emotional Intelligence (Personal: 3/10 → 7/10)

**Why:** When Harry critiqued my delegation, I felt attacked and shut down. This damages relationships essential for scaling.

**Values:** "Pause and reflect"—three breaths before responding; viewing feedback as growth data.

**Activities:** Daily mindfulness (10 mins); weekly feedback reflection journal; practising non-defensive responses in team meetings.

**Success:** 70% constructive response rate by March 2026 (measured through Harry's weekly feedback logs documenting my response patterns).

---

## 3. Mentoring Others (Team: 3/10 → 8/10)

**Why:** I've felt frustrated when Sam doesn't approach tasks exactly as I would, then realised I haven't taught him my thinking. This isn't delegation—it's abdication.

**Values:** Explicit thinking transfer, tolerating short-term inefficiency, celebrating Sam's independence.

**Activities:** Implementing GROW model in weekly Sam mentoring sessions starting January; documenting thinking processes; TE mentoring workshop in February.

**Success:** Sam independently managing 15 partnerships by April 2026 (tracked through weekly independence assessment: 0=needs direction, 1=partial independence, 2=fully independent).

---

## 4. Team Communication (Team: 4/10 → 8/10)

**Why:** Only 40% of my recommendations are implemented. When Leo challenged my partnership proposal, I pushed harder rather than understanding his perspective.

**Values:** "Listen first"—three clarifying questions before advocating; recognising resistance as valuable data.

**Activities:** Completing Crucial Conversations online course by February; weekly structured listening practice; recording communication patterns.

**Success:** 80% recommendation adoption by April 2026 (tracked through team meeting minutes: recommendations made vs. implemented within 2 weeks).

---

## 5. Service Design & Delivery (Venture: 2/10 → 7/10)

**Why:** I have 70 partnerships despite targeting 1,000. When I presented my model, David pointed out I'd designed it for my convenience, not agency needs. Genuine value requires co-design.

**Values:** User journey mapping, co-creation workshops, rapid prototyping, iterating based on genuine feedback.

**Activities:** Completing IDEO/Coursera Service Design course by February; co-creation sessions with 5 current partners in January-February; iterating model based on feedback.

**Success:** 150 validated partnerships by April 2026 (measured through partnership count + 60-day retention rate; tracked in partnership dashboard).

---

## 6. Financial Management (Venture: 3/10 → 7/10)

**Why:** I've focused on systems without revenue. When Leo asked about pricing, I felt exposed—this is a hobby, not a business. Sustainable scaling requires value-capture thinking.

**Values:** Ethical pricing based on delivered value; sustainable growth; transparent stewardship.

**Activities:** Completing TE financial modelling workshop in January; building dashboard tracking CAC, LTV, margins weekly; pricing strategy mentorship in February.

**Success:** £5K monthly revenue by April 2026 (measured through revenue dashboard with monthly profitability review).
