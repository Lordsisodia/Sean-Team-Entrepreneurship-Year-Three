# Section 08: Competencies I Will Develop

> Detailed breakdown of six competencies selected for development during Q4, aligned to my learning objectives and development activities.

---

## Overview

Six competencies for Q4 (January–April 2026): **2 Venture**, **2 Team**, **2 Personal**. Each links to Q4 activities with measurable indicators.

---

## 1. Values Alignment (Personal: 4/10 → 8/10)

**Why:** David suggested cutting corners on partner vetting; I stayed silent. Sustainable partnerships need a values-based decision framework.

**Values:** Core principles (integrity, mutual value, long-term focus) with decision trees for dilemmas.

**Activities:** Weekly values reflection; creating framework with TE mentor; reviewing all partnership decisions against framework.

**Success:** 80% values-consistent decisions by February 2026 (weekly decision audit with TE mentor, documented).

---

## 2. Emotional Intelligence (Personal: 3/10 → 7/10)

**Why:** Harry critiqued my delegation; I felt attacked and shut down. This damages scaling relationships.

**Values:** "Pause and reflect"—three breaths before responding; viewing feedback as growth data.

**Activities:** Daily mindfulness (10 mins); weekly feedback reflection; seeking TE peer input; practising non-defensive responses.

**Success:** 70% constructive responses by March 2026 (weekly TE peer feedback, Google Sheet).

---

## 3. Mentoring Others (Team: 3/10 → 8/10)

**Why:** Working with Sam, I've felt frustrated when his approach differs, then realised I haven't taught him my thinking. This isn't delegation—it's abdication.

**Values:** Explicit thinking transfer, tolerating short-term inefficiency, celebrating Sam's independence.

**Activities:** Weekly sessions (GROW model); documenting thinking processes; observing and providing feedback; TE mentoring workshops.

**Success:** Sam independently managing 50 partnerships by April 2026 (monthly independence assessment, project dashboard).

---

## 4. Team Communication (Team: 4/10 → 8/10)

**Why:** Only 40% of my recommendations are implemented. When Leo challenged my proposal, I pushed harder rather than understanding his perspective.

**Values:** "Listen first"—three clarifying questions before advocating; recognising resistance as valuable data.

**Activities:** Crucial Conversations training (complete by February 2026); weekly structured listening practice; recording and analysing patterns; TE coaching on influence.

**Success:** 80% recommendation adoption by February 2026 (biweekly team adoption metrics, meeting minutes).

---

## 5. Service Design & Delivery (Venture: 2/10 → 7/10)

**Why:** Zero partnerships despite targeting 1,000. When I presented my model, David pointed out I'd designed it for my convenience, not agency needs. Genuine value requires co-design.

**Values:** User journey mapping, co-creation workshops, rapid prototyping, iterating based on genuine feedback.

**Activities:** IDEO/Coursera Service Design course (complete by March 2026); pilots with 10 agencies; weekly co-creation; analysing successful programmes (HubSpot, Salesforce).

**Success:** 100 validated partnerships by April 2026 (weekly dashboard tracking 60%+ retention, TE mentor validation quarterly).

---

## 6. Financial Management (Venture: 3/10 → 7/10)

**Why:** I've focused on systems without revenue. When Leo asked about pricing, I felt exposed—this is a hobby, not a business. Sustainable scaling requires value-capture thinking.

**Values:** Ethical pricing based on delivered value; sustainable growth; transparent stewardship.

**Activities:** TE financial modelling workshops (complete by February 2026); dashboard tracking CAC, LTV, margins weekly; working with mentors on pricing; cash flow forecasting.

**Success:** £5K monthly revenue by April 2026 (weekly dashboard tracking MRR, churn, margin, monthly review with mentor).

---

## Integration with Q4 Development

**Connections:** Values + Emotional Intelligence (ethical decisions require regulation), Mentoring + Communication (delegation needs clarity), Service Design + Financial Management (sustainable partnerships require viable economics).

**Timeline:** January builds foundations (values, EI practice, financial modelling), February-March scales (mentoring, communication, service design), April integrates all (revenue, validation).

**Measurement:** Binary metrics tracked weekly in Notion dashboard, monthly reviews with TE mentors, portfolio outputs demonstrating application in real partnerships.
