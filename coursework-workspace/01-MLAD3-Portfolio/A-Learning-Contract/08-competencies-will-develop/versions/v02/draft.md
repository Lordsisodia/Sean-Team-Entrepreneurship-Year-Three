# Section 08: Competencies I Will Develop

> Detailed breakdown of the six competencies selected for development during Q4, aligned to my learning objectives and development activities.

---

## Overview

I have selected six competencies for focused development during Q4 (January–March 2025). These are deliberately balanced across three domains:

- **2 Venture Competencies** - Directly supporting business growth
- **2 Team Competencies** - Strengthening collaborative effectiveness
- **2 Personal Competencies** - Enhancing strategic judgment and personal effectiveness

Each competency links explicitly to my Q4 development activities and includes measurable success indicators.

---

## 1. Strategic Leadership (Venture)

### Why This Competency?

Leading a multi-stakeholder partnership venture requires transitioning from founder to strategic leader. I must delegate operational responsibility to Sam whilst maintaining strategic coherence across the partnership network. My instinct is to do everything myself because "I'll spend more time explaining than doing it myself"—this mindset is unsustainable at scale.

### Values & Ethics Dimension

Leadership in this context centres on ethical delegation—empowering Sam with genuine decision-making authority whilst retaining accountability for venture outcomes. This requires transparent communication about expectations, regular check-ins to provide support without micromanagement, and creating space for Sam to shape the operational direction. It means resisting the urge to step back in when things aren't done my way, recognising that effective delegation tolerates short-term inefficiency for long-term capability development.

### Development Activities

- **Weekly Delegation Sessions**: Structured sessions with Sam focused on building capability, not just assigning tasks
- **Team Capability Assessments**: Understanding Sam's strengths and growth areas for more effective delegation
- **Documentation Framework**: Creating clear systems documentation that enables independent operational management
- **Mentorship**: Learning from TE alumni who have successfully built and managed teams

### Success Indicators

- Sam independently managing 50+ partnerships by March 2026
- Strategic review meetings focusing on direction rather than task status
- Documented delegation framework referenced and refined through practice

## 2. Service Design & Delivery (Venture)

### Why This Competency?

Designing value propositions that serve both students and businesses requires systematic service design thinking. I currently have zero agency partnerships yet my goal is 1,000—this gap between aspiration and capability requires structured development. I need to move beyond intuitive offerings to evidence-backed service models that create measurable mutual value.

### Values & Ethics Dimension

Service design grounded in co-creation ensures we design with, not for, our users. This means actively seeking input from student partners and business clients, recognising power dynamics in consultation processes, and iterating based on genuine feedback rather than assumptions. Ethical service design acknowledges that early adopters are taking risks on unproven offerings and requires honouring their trust with responsive support and honest communication about what is and isn't working.

### Development Activities

- **Partnership Mentorship**: Learning from others who have built partner networks at scale
- **Pilot Testing**: Running small-scale pilots with 10 agencies to validate value propositions
- **Service Blueprinting**: Creating end-to-end journey maps for student and business partner experiences
- **Framework Application**: Studying successful partner programs (HubSpot, Shopify, WordPress VIP) to inform design

### Success Indicators

- 100 validated partnerships by January 2026
- Documented service blueprints identifying pain points and opportunity areas
- Tested value proposition canvases validated by early partners

## 3. Professional Communication (Team)

### Why This Competency?

Working with Sam, Leo, David, and Harry requires adapting communication styles across different contexts—operations, strategy, and stakeholder engagement. The gap between my technical recommendations and their implementation isn't about the quality of my ideas—it's about my ability to influence and persuade. My 40% team adoption rate challenge indicates that I'm good at information transfer but weak at influence.

### Values & Ethics Dimension

Ethical communication means practising active listening, particularly when perspectives differ. It involves acknowledging the expertise others bring, being transparent about constraints and uncertainties, and building consensus through genuine dialogue rather than persuasion tactics. This means starting with clarity on my own motives before attempting to influence others, and recognising that resistance to my ideas may reflect valid concerns about implementation feasibility or strategic alignment rather than misunderstanding.

### Development Activities

- **Communication Skills Training**: Focused on stakeholder influence, not just information transfer
- **Recording and Review**: Capturing interactions to identify patterns in communication effectiveness
- **TE Workshop Leadership**: Building influence through teaching and facilitating
- **Framework Application**: Applying "Start with Heart" principle from Crucial Conversations

### Success Indicators

- Adoption rate increases from 40% to 80% by February 2026
- Documented communication preferences for Sam, Leo, David, and Harry
- Preparation frameworks used for strategic conversations

## 4. Financial Management (Team)

### Why This Competency?

Deploying team resources (people, time, money) across ventures requires financial literacy beyond basic budgeting. My natural inclination is to build and optimise systems, but systems without revenue are hobbies, not businesses. I need to develop understanding of cash flow, investment decisions, and resource optimisation within a multi-venture portfolio to move from planning to execution.

### Values & Ethics Dimension

Ethical financial stewardship means making allocation decisions transparently, with clear rationale communicated to the team. It involves balancing venture needs fairly, avoiding over-commitment to high-risk opportunities, and maintaining reserves for sustainability. It means being honest about financial constraints rather than overpromising, and recognising that aggressive revenue targets shouldn't compromise ethical service delivery or exploit partner relationships.

### Development Activities

- **Financial Modelling**: Building cash flow projections and understanding unit economics (CAC, LTV, gross margins)
- **Pricing Strategy**: Developing frameworks for profitable pricing decisions
- **Financial Dashboard**: Creating real-time tracking of key financial metrics
- **Mentorship**: Working with TE mentors who have built profitable businesses

### Success Indicators

- £5K+ monthly revenue by December 2025
- Working financial models used to inform venture decisions
- Documented allocation rationale for major resource deployments

## 5. Self-Management (Personal)

### Why This Competency?

Juggling multiple high-stakes projects simultaneously requires effective personal organisation in complex environments. My September data revealed that I spent 60% of my time on technical work that could have been delegated or deferred, and only 20% on partnership outreach—the one activity that actually drives my exit strategy forward. This quantified self-awareness is driving the need for systematic self-management.

### Values & Ethics Dimension

Ethical self-management means recognising that my time is a finite resource that affects others—when I misallocate my time, I'm not just affecting my own productivity, I'm impacting the team's ability to execute and our partners' ability to rely on us. It requires honesty about my own patterns of avoidance (the 6-week internal app mistake) and willingness to prioritise strategic work over urgent but unimportant tasks, even when the strategic work is more uncomfortable.

### Development Activities

- **Time-Blocking via Internal App**: Systematic allocation of time to highest-impact activities
- **Weekly Priority Reviews**: Ensuring alignment between time allocation and strategic objectives
- **Productivity Frameworks**: Implementing structured approaches to complex prioritisation
- **Objective Tracking**: Using data to identify and correct misalignment

### Success Indicators

- 80% time allocation alignment with strategic priorities by December 2025
- Reduced time on delegable technical work (from 60% to <30%)
- Increased time on partnership outreach (from 20% to >50%)

## 6. Entrepreneurial Judgment (Personal)

### Why This Competency?

Evaluating venture opportunities, team dynamics, and strategic options requires structured analytical thinking. My 6-week internal app mistake wasn't about technical execution—it was about strategic prioritisation. I spent time on tasks that were urgent but not important, failing to ask: "If this task didn't exist, would I create it today?" I must develop frameworks for questioning assumptions and making reasoned judgments under uncertainty.

### Values & Ethics Dimension

Ethical entrepreneurial judgment involves examining my own positions of privilege and how these shape decision-making. It means seeking out diverse perspectives, particularly those likely to be marginalised, and being willing to reconsider deeply-held assumptions in light of new evidence. It requires acknowledging that my intuitive judgments may reflect bias rather than insight, and creating systematic checks to prevent ego-driven decisions that waste team resources or partner trust.

### Development Activities

- **Weekly Strategic Reviews**: Stepping back from execution to assess resource allocation
- **Decision Frameworks**: Applying structured tools (Eisenhower Matrix, pre-mortems, scenario analysis)
- **Mentorship**: Learning from experienced entrepreneurs about decision-making under uncertainty
- **Bias Awareness**: Regular reflection identifying cognitive biases in team decisions

### Success Indicators

- Partnership/client apps completed by December 2025
- Documented decision frameworks applied to Q4 strategic choices
- Reflection journal identifying biases and mitigation strategies
