# Section 08: Competencies I Will Develop

> Detailed breakdown of the six competencies selected for development during Q4, aligned to my learning objectives and development activities.

---

## Overview

I have selected six competencies for focused development during Q4 (January–March 2025), deliberately balanced: **2 Venture** (business growth), **2 Team** (collaborative effectiveness), **2 Personal** (strategic judgment). Each links explicitly to Q4 development activities with measurable success indicators.

---

## 1. Strategic Leadership (Venture)

**Why:** Leading multi-stakeholder partnerships requires transitioning from founder to strategic leader. I must delegate operational responsibility to Sam whilst maintaining strategic coherence. My instinct—"I'll spend more time explaining than doing"—is unsustainable at scale.

**Values:** Ethical delegation means empowering Sam with genuine decision-making authority whilst retaining accountability, requiring transparent communication, support without micromanagement, and tolerating short-term inefficiency for long-term capability development.

**Activities:** Weekly delegation sessions building Sam's capability; systems documentation enabling independent operational management; learning from TE alumni who have successfully built teams.

**Success:** Sam independently managing 50+ partnerships by March 2026; strategic reviews focusing on direction, not task status.

---

## 2. Service Design & Delivery (Venture)

**Why:** I currently have zero agency partnerships yet target 1,000—this gap requires structured development. I need evidence-backed service models creating measurable mutual value.

**Values:** Co-creation ensures we design with, not for, users. This means actively seeking input, recognising power dynamics, iterating based on genuine feedback. Early adopters take risks and deserve honest communication.

**Activities:** Pilots with 10 agencies validating value propositions; journey maps for student and partner experiences; studying successful partner programs (HubSpot, Shopify).

**Success:** 100 validated partnerships by January 2026; tested value proposition canvases validated by early partners.

---

## 3. Professional Communication (Team)

**Why:** Working with Sam, Leo, David, and Harry requires adapting communication across contexts. My 40% team adoption rate indicates I'm good at information transfer but weak at influence.

**Values:** Ethical communication means active listening, acknowledging others' expertise, building consensus through genuine dialogue. Resistance may reflect valid concerns about feasibility or alignment.

**Activities:** Communication skills training focused on stakeholder influence; applying "Start with Heart" principle from Crucial Conversations; building influence through teaching TE workshops.

**Success:** Adoption rate increases from 40% to 80% by February 2026; documented communication preferences for team members.

---

## 4. Financial Management (Team)

**Why:** Deploying team resources requires financial literacy beyond basic budgeting. Systems without revenue are hobbies. I need cash flow understanding and resource optimisation to move from planning to execution.

**Values:** Ethical stewardship means transparent allocation decisions with clear rationale, balancing venture needs fairly, avoiding over-commitment to high-risk opportunities, and recognising that aggressive targets shouldn't compromise ethical service delivery.

**Activities:** Building cash flow projections and understanding unit economics; creating real-time tracking of key metrics; working with TE mentors who have built profitable businesses.

**Success:** £5K+ monthly revenue by December 2025; working financial models used to inform venture decisions.

---

## 5. Self-Management (Personal)

**Why:** September data revealed I spent 60% of time on delegable technical work and only 20% on partnership outreach. This quantified self-awareness drives systematic self-management.

**Values:** My time affects others—misallocation impacts team execution and partners' reliance. It requires honesty about avoidance patterns and willingness to prioritise strategic work over urgent but unimportant tasks.

**Activities:** Systematic time allocation to highest-impact activities via internal app; weekly priority reviews ensuring alignment; using data to identify and correct misalignment.

**Success:** 80% time allocation alignment by December 2025; reduced delegable technical work (60% → <30%); increased partnership outreach (20% → >50%).

---

## 6. Entrepreneurial Judgment (Personal)

**Why:** My 6-week internal app mistake wasn't technical execution—it was strategic prioritisation. I must develop frameworks for questioning assumptions and making reasoned judgments under uncertainty.

**Values:** Examining positions of privilege and seeking diverse perspectives. Acknowledging intuitive judgments may reflect bias, creating systematic checks to prevent ego-driven decisions wasting team resources.

**Activities:** Weekly strategic reviews assessing resource allocation; applying structured decision tools (Eisenhower Matrix, pre-mortems); learning from experienced entrepreneurs about decision-making.

**Success:** Partnership/client apps completed by December 2025; documented decision frameworks applied to Q4 strategic choices.

---

## Integration with Q4 Development

These competencies integrate: Strategic Leadership + Professional Communication (effective delegation requires clear protocols), Service Design + Self-Management (scaling partnerships requires systematic time allocation), Financial Management + Entrepreneurial Judgment (sound resource allocation benefits from structured analysis).

Timeline: October-November builds foundations (Service Design pilots, Financial Management dashboard, Self-Management time-blocking), December-February scales what works (Strategic Leadership delegation accelerates, Professional Communication influence increases adoption), March-April integrates all competencies.

All competencies connect directly to Section 07 activities and will be evidenced through portfolio outputs.
