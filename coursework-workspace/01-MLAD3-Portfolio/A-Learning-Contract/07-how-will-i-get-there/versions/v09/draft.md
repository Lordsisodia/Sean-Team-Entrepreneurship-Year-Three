# MLAD3 Learning Contract - Question 4: How will I get there?

To build my partnership-based AI automation empire, I must develop six critical competencies through specific resources, behaviours, and habits. My Team Review 1 feedback from Sam, Leo, David, and Harry in October 2025 revealed a consistent theme: I'm doing the right work but I'm the bottleneck. The solution isn't to work differently—it's to offload intelligence to AI and systems.

**Values Alignment** (Self-Development: 4/10 → 8/10)

During October vetting of a London marketing agency for a £25K automation contract, David suggested accepting despite technical readiness concerns. I felt uneasy but stayed silent. The client later churned. I'm clarifying my core values—integrity, mutual value, long-term focus—using Daft's ethical decision-making framework. Daily: log values-consistent decisions. Weekly: Harry reviews my log. Target: 80% by February.

**Emotional Intelligence** (Self-Development: 3/10 → 7/10)

Harry observed that I "love feedback, especially negative feedback because it gives me more to learn from"—this genuine growth mindset is my foundation. However, my response to feedback reveals room for growth. When Harry critiqued my management style during our weekly one-to-ones, I initially felt defensive before recognizing the value in his perspective. I'm practising "pause and reflect"—three breaths before responding. Resource: TE coaching sessions. Daily: before morning meetings, I practice the 3-breath pause. Target: 70% by March.

**Mentoring Others** (Team-Development: 3/10 → 8/10)

Sam provided direct feedback in October: "We currently have a very loose delegation and management style. We catch up to see what needs doing, allocate tasks, then just do them, talking only if we need help." He explicitly requested: "You should probably ask me for help more often just so I'm kept in the loop. This would allow you to provide insight into how to do things more efficiently."

The core issue: Sam needs to use AI more effectively to delegate and handle tasks independently. My current approach—intensive training sessions like the 4-hour "zero to hero" Claude Code session (Supabase, Vercel, full-stack apps)—creates strong results but dependency. This isn't sustainable at scale. I'll implement weekly GROW model mentoring sessions, documenting my thinking processes explicitly to capture knowledge in systems, not just heads. Resource: TE Mentoring in Residence programme. Impact: Sam gains autonomy; I gain scalability. Habit: Friday 2-3 PM mentoring blocks with structured check-ins, tolerating short-term inefficiency while celebrating independence. Target: Sam managing 50 partnerships by April.

**Team Communication** (Team-Development: 4/10 → 8/10)

Leo's feedback: More than 40% of my bronze/silver/gold tier proposal got implemented—"great idea," he said. But the main issue: "You're too busy. You don't have enough time to talk to people because you're working so hard." My hyperfocus means I spend all my time on single tasks, creating a communication bottleneck. When I proposed tiered pricing, Leo challenged whether this served agency needs or my categorisation preference. I pushed technical arguments rather than understanding his perspective first.

I'm adopting "listen first"—three clarifying questions before advocating. Resource: TE influence workshop series. Daily: meeting prep includes identifying three questions to ask. Impact: Leo and David feel heard, not overruled. Target: 80% adoption by February.

**Service Design & Delivery** (Venture: 2/10 → 7/10)

David's direct feedback: "Busy agency owners won't complete technical setup themselves." I agreed 100%: "That's why we've made setup very easy. It can be completed over time, not all at once. Log in, create app, connect phone number. Agent even guides them on their own time." We pushed a product update forward to address this feedback.

Partnership progress: "Poor." We've vetted 12-15 partners, have 3 active, but closed others because we're specifically targeting e-commerce clients. David suggested: "Find specific groups and communities where agency owners hang out and message them directly there." This is now my outreach strategy. Resources: *Service Design Handbook*, TE service design mentorship, e-commerce agency communities. Habit: weekly co-creation sessions with 2 agencies, user journey mapping after each. Target: 100 validated partnerships by January.

**Financial Management** (Venture: 3/10 → 7/10)

Leo exposed a gap in October: "No cost structure, no unit economics" for £5K implementations. I felt exposed—this looked like a hobby, not a business. Everyone from first principles to AI to people around me said: charge a retainer. New model: Upfront setup fee (£1,000 → £2,000 target) + monthly retainer (£50-£200/month for 24/7 on-call dev services, worth £1,000-£2,000/month in retrospect). I need training data from clients, so exact pricing is secondary to cash flow right now. Resources: SaaS pricing courses, TE finance mentorship. Habit: Friday 4 PM dashboard review tracking CAC, LTV, and margins weekly. Impact: transparent financials enable Sam and Leo to make informed decisions. Target: £5K monthly revenue by December.

**AI Systematization** (Venture: Critical Enabler)

Leo's first-principles conclusion: (1) Need most efficient off-boarded intelligence possible, (2) Cheapest/most efficient option is AI, (3) Must train AI to handle specific tasks or systemize for repeatable execution. Harry echoed: "Be less busy. Offload intelligence and compute so it can be handled elsewhere. Only you can lay it out and plan it—need things working in background."

This is my highest leverage activity. Every hour spent systematizing my knowledge into AI agents or documentation saves 10+ hours of future 1-on-1 training. Resources: OpenAI documentation, Claude Code, agent frameworks. Habit: Daily systematization block (9-11am) before client work. Target: 50% of common tasks handled by AI by March.

**Timeline & Accountability**

October-November builds foundations through daily habits, December-February scales proven approaches with AI support, March-April integrates all competencies. Weekly Friday 4 PM reviews with Sam ensure accountability—progress shared, adjustments made together. Harry, Leo, David, and I have ongoing 1-on-1 feedback loops catching issues early.
