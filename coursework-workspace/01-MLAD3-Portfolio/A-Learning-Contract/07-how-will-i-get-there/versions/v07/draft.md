# MLAD3 Learning Contract - Question 4: How will I get there?

To build my partnership-based AI automation empire, I must develop six critical competencies.

**Values Alignment** (Self-Development: 4/10 → 8/10)

I've struggled with ethical tension. During our October vetting of a London-based marketing agency for a £25K automation contract, David suggested accepting their partnership application despite concerns about their technical readiness. I felt uneasy but stayed silent. The client later churned. I'm clarifying my core values and creating a decision framework. Target: values-consistent decisions 80% of the time by February.

**Emotional Intelligence** (Self-Development: 3/10 → 7/10)

I react defensively to feedback. During our weekly one-to-one in late September, Harry critiqued my approach to delegating client onboarding tasks, noting I was micromanaging Sam's work on the NHS outreach campaign rather than trusting him to learn. I felt attacked and shut down, missing valuable insight. This pattern limits my growth. I'm practising "pause and reflect". Target: constructive feedback responses 70% of the time by March.

**Mentoring Others** (Team-Development: 3/10 → 8/10)

I default to doing everything myself. Working with Sam on our Q4 property sector partnership outreach campaign targeting 50 UK estate agencies, I've felt frustrated when he doesn't approach tasks exactly as I would, then realised I haven't taught him my thinking process. During a mid-October review, Sam admitted he was guessing at outreach strategy because I'd never documented our qualification criteria. This isn't delegation—it's abdication. I'll implement weekly mentoring sessions. Target: Sam managing 50 partnerships by April.

**Team Communication** (Team-Development: 4/10 → 8/10)

Only 40% of my team recommendations are implemented. When I proposed a tiered partnership structure (bronze/silver/gold access levels) during our November strategy meeting, Leo challenged whether this aligned with agency needs or just my desire for neat categorisation. I felt dismissed and pushed harder with technical arguments rather than understanding his perspective, turning collaboration into conflict. The proposal stalled. I'm adopting a "listen first" approach. Target: 80% recommendation adoption by February.

**Service Design & Delivery** (Venture: 2/10 → 7/10)

I have zero agency partnerships despite targeting 1,000. When I presented my partnership model design at our September planning session—a self-service portal requiring agencies to configure their own automation stacks—David pointed out I'd designed it for my convenience, not agency needs. He asked: "When have you ever seen a busy agency owner complete a complex technical setup themselves?" I felt embarrassed but grateful he caught it before we built it. I'll pilot with 10 agencies. Target: 100 validated partnerships by January.

**Financial Management** (Venture: 3/10 → 7/10)

I've focused on systems without considering revenue. During our October budget review, Leo asked about our pricing model for the white-label automation services: "What's our margin on a typical £5K agency implementation?" I felt exposed because I hadn't thought about it—no cost structure, no unit economics, no pricing strategy. I'm shifting to value-capture thinking. I'll build a dashboard tracking CAC, LTV, and margins weekly. Target: £5K monthly revenue by December.

**Timeline & Review**

October-November builds foundations, December-February scales proven approaches, March-April integrates all competencies. Every Friday at 4 PM, I review progress.
