# MLAD3 Learning Contract - Question 4: How will I get there?

To build my partnership-based AI automation empire, I must develop six critical competencies.

**Values Alignment** (Self-Development: 4/10 → 8/10)

I've struggled with ethical tension. During October vetting of a London marketing agency for a £25K automation contract, David suggested accepting their partnership application despite concerns about their technical readiness. I felt uneasy but stayed silent. The client later churned. I'm clarifying my core values—integrity, mutual value, long-term focus—and creating a decision framework for ethical dilemmas with decision trees for partnership decisions. Target: values-consistent decisions 80% of the time by February.

**Emotional Intelligence** (Self-Development: 3/10 → 7/10)

I react defensively to feedback. During our weekly one-to-one in late September, Harry critiqued my approach to delegating client onboarding tasks, noting I was micromanaging Sam's work on the NHS outreach campaign rather than trusting him to learn. I felt attacked and shut down, missing valuable insight. This pattern limits my growth and damages relationships essential for scaling. I'm practising "pause and reflect"—three breaths before responding, viewing feedback as growth data rather than personal criticism. Target: constructive responses 70% of the time by March.

**Mentoring Others** (Team-Development: 3/10 → 8/10)

I default to doing everything myself. On Q4 property outreach targeting 50 UK agencies, Sam guessed at strategy because I'd never documented qualification criteria. This isn't delegation—it's abdication. I'll implement weekly mentoring sessions using the GROW model, document my thinking processes explicitly, and tolerate short-term inefficiency while celebrating Sam's independence. Target: Sam managing 50 partnerships by April.

**Team Communication** (Team-Development: 4/10 → 8/10)

Only 40% of my recommendations are implemented. When I proposed bronze/silver/gold tiers in November, Leo challenged whether this served agency needs or just my desire for neat categorisation. I pushed harder with technical arguments rather than understanding his perspective. The proposal stalled. I'm adopting "listen first"—three clarifying questions before advocating, recognising resistance as valuable data rather than opposition. Target: 80% adoption by February.

**Service Design & Delivery** (Venture: 2/10 → 7/10)

I have zero agency partnerships despite targeting 1,000. In September, David critiqued my self-service portal design: "When have you ever seen a busy agency owner complete complex technical setup themselves?" I'd designed for my convenience, not agency needs. Genuine value requires co-design. I'll run co-creation sessions with 10 agencies, conduct user journey mapping, and iterate based on genuine feedback through rapid prototyping. Target: 100 validated partnerships by January.

**Financial Management** (Venture: 3/10 → 7/10)

I've focused on systems without revenue. In October, Leo asked about pricing for £5K implementations: no cost structure, no unit economics. I felt exposed—this is a hobby, not a business. Sustainable scaling requires value-capture thinking. I'll build a dashboard tracking CAC, LTV, and margins weekly, and develop ethical pricing based on delivered value with transparent stewardship. Target: £5K monthly revenue by December.

**Timeline & Review**

October-November builds foundations, December-February scales proven approaches, March-April integrates all competencies. Every Friday at 4 PM, I review progress against these six competency targets and adjust my approach.
