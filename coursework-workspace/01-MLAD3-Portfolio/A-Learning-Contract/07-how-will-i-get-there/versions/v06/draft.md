# MLAD3 Learning Contract - Question 4: How will I get there?

To achieve my exit strategy of 1,000 agency partnerships, I must develop six competencies addressing gaps revealed through difficult team experiences.

**Values Alignment** (Self-Development: 4/10 → 8/10)

I've struggled with ethical tension between growth and quality. During October vetting of a London marketing agency for a £25K contract, David suggested accepting despite technical readiness concerns. I stayed silent, compromising standards. The client churned due to poor implementation. I'm clarifying core values and creating a decision framework. Target: 80% values-consistent decisions by February.

**Emotional Intelligence** (Self-Development: 3/10 → 7/10)

I react defensively to feedback. During our weekly one-to-one in late September, Harry critiqued my delegation on the NHS campaign, noting I was micromanaging Sam. I felt attacked and shut down. This limits growth. I'm practising "pause and reflect"—three breaths before responding. Target: 70% constructive responses by March.

**Mentoring Others** (Team-Development: 3/10 → 8/10)

I default to doing everything myself. Working with Sam on our Q4 property sector campaign targeting 50 UK estate agencies, I've felt frustrated when he doesn't approach tasks exactly as I would, then realised I haven't taught him my process. During mid-October review, Sam admitted guessing at strategy because I'd never documented qualification criteria. This isn't delegation—it's abdication. I'll implement weekly mentoring sessions. Target: Sam independently managing 50 partnerships by April.

**Team Communication** (Team-Development: 4/10 → 8/10)

Only 40% of my recommendations are implemented. When I proposed tiered structure (bronze/silver/gold access levels) during November strategy meeting, Leo challenged whether this aligned with agency needs or my desire for categorisation. I felt dismissed and pushed harder with technical arguments rather than understanding his perspective. The proposal stalled. I'm adopting "listen first"—asking questions before advocating positions. Target: 80% recommendation adoption by February.

**Service Design & Delivery** (Venture: 2/10 → 7/10)

I have zero partnerships despite targeting 1,000. When I presented my self-service portal at September planning, David pointed out I'd designed it for my convenience, not agency needs. He asked: "When have you ever seen a busy agency owner complete a complex technical setup themselves?" I'm committed to genuine value. I'll pilot with 10 agencies using service design frameworks. Target: 100 validated partnerships by April.

**Financial Management** (Venture: 3/10 → 7/10)

I've focused on systems without considering revenue. During October budget review, Leo asked about pricing for £5K white-label services: "What's our margin?" I felt exposed—no cost structure, no unit economics. This is a hobby, not a business. I'm shifting to value-capture thinking with ethical pricing. I'll build a dashboard tracking CAC, LTV, and margins. Target: £5K monthly revenue by April.

**Timeline**

October-November builds foundations (values, emotional intelligence, financial systems), December-February scales (mentoring, communication), March-April integrates all competencies. I review progress against binary metrics every Friday at 4 PM.
