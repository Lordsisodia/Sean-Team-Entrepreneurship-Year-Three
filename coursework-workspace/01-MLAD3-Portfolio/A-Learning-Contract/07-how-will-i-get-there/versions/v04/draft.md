# MLAD3 Learning Contract - Question 4: How will I get there?

To build my partnership-based AI automation empire, I must develop across six critical competencies. Each addresses a gap identified through difficult team experiences and connects directly to my exit strategy of 1,000 agency partnerships.

**Values Alignment** (Self-Development: 4/10 → 8/10)

I've struggled with ethical tension between growth and quality. When David suggested cutting corners on partner vetting, I felt uneasy but stayed silent, compromising my standards. To achieve sustainable partnerships, I'm clarifying my core values and creating a decision framework. Target: making values-consistent decisions 80% of the time by February.

**Emotional Intelligence** (Self-Development: 3/10 → 7/10)

I react defensively to feedback. When Harry critiqued my delegation approach, I felt attacked and shut down, missing valuable insight. This pattern limits my growth and damages relationships essential for scaling. I'm practising "pause and reflect"—taking three breaths before responding. Target: responding constructively 70% of the time by March.

**Mentoring Others** (Team-Development: 3/10 → 8/10)

I default to doing everything myself. Working with Sam on partnership outreach, I've felt frustrated when he doesn't approach tasks exactly as I would, then realised I haven't taught him my thinking process. This isn't delegation—it's abdication. To scale operations beyond my personal capacity, I'll implement weekly mentoring sessions. Target: Sam independently managing 50 partnerships by April.

**Team Communication** (Team-Development: 4/10 → 8/10)

Only 40% of my team recommendations are implemented. When Leo challenged my partnership structure proposal, I felt dismissed and pushed harder rather than understanding his perspective, turning collaboration into conflict. I'm adopting a "listen first" approach—asking questions before advocating positions. Target: 80% adoption by February.

**Service Design & Delivery** (Venture: 2/10 → 7/10)

I have zero agency partnerships despite targeting 1,000. When I presented my partnership model, David pointed out I'd designed it for my convenience, not agency needs—I felt embarrassed but grateful he caught it early. I'm committed to creating genuine value, not extraction. I'll pilot with 10 agencies using service design frameworks. Target: 100 validated partnerships by January.

**Financial Management** (Venture: 3/10 → 7/10)

I've focused on systems without considering revenue. When Leo asked about our pricing model, I felt exposed because I hadn't thought about it—this is a hobby, not a business. I'm shifting to value-capture thinking with ethical pricing and sustainable growth. I'll build a dashboard tracking CAC, LTV, and margins weekly. Target: £5K monthly revenue by December.

Timeline: October-November builds foundations (values, emotional intelligence, financial systems), December-February scales proven approaches (mentoring Sam, team communication), March-April integrates all competencies. Every Friday at 4 PM, I review progress against binary metrics.
