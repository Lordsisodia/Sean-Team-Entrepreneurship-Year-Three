## Perfectionism as Avoidance (November 2025)

When Sam asked why I was refining automation code instead of reaching out to agencies, I felt defensive. Two days automating a 30-minute task delayed our partnership app by another week. Sam's frustration was justified. This revealed a painful pattern: I choose comfortable technical work over uncomfortable interpersonal tasks. I realize I'm afraid of rejection—coding never rejects me, but people might. There's an ethical tension: my value of "maximum impact" justifies long-term leverage investment, yet clients waiting deserve urgency.

After Sam's feedback, I implemented a 24-hour response rule for all partnership inquiries. In January 2026, I caught myself starting to refactor code and stopped immediately, sending an outreach email instead. The client responded within an hour. This shows that pattern is interruptible when I create concrete rules that override INTJ perfectionism. The question I now grapple with: why does INTJ certainty preference feel safer than relationship uncertainty, even when the latter creates the growth I claim to value? My Temperament Assessment (INTJ) and Strengths Assessment (conclusion-driven thinking) confirm this preference, but recognizing it doesn't explain it away.

## Delegation as Control (November 2025)

When building a portfolio for a major client, I insisted on handling it myself despite being overwhelmed. I told myself I'd spend more time explaining than doing it. The reality? Sam could have handled it, and the project took two weeks longer than necessary. This pattern—driven by my belief that "I'll do it best"—creates bottlenecks.

Ethical issue: withholding delegation denies Sam growth. Team growth conflicts with hoarding work. My Learning Styles (Score: 114) shows deep independent processing, counterproductive in delegation. Authentic leadership means releasing control—trusting others develops capability faster.

This pattern resurfaced in mid-October. When Sam admitted, "I was guessing at outreach strategy because you'd never documented our qualification criteria," I realized I hadn't delegated—I'd abdicated. My control left Sam operating without guidance, creating exactly what I feared: slower outcomes and frustrated team members. During our October feedback session, I committed to weekly delegation planning. Since then, Sam has independently qualified 7 agencies in January, validating this approach.

## Environmental Design Over Willpower (January 2026)

Successfully eliminating cannabis use upon arriving in Bali revealed a key insight: environmental factors trump willpower. Previous attempts failed because I tried to change behaviour without changing context. The new location broke established patterns.

But why did I tolerate environments that undermine my values for months before making a change? My Motivation at Work Assessment shows high scores in Development and Control, yet these same drivers prevented me from designing an environment that supports my stated values of health and performance. This gap between intention and action—knowing what's right but not doing it—is my core challenge. Integrity means designing environments that make desired behaviours inevitable, not eventually.
