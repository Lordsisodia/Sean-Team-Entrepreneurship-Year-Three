## üìã UNDERTAKINGS BREAKDOWN

### Learning from Others (LfO) - 6 Undertakings

**What They Are:**
Learning experiences where you gain knowledge, skills, or insights from other people.

**Examples:**
- Mentor meetings with structured learning goals
- Shadowing professionals or entrepreneurs
- Networking events with specific learning outcomes
- Workshops, courses, or training programs
- Informational interviews with industry experts
- Coaching sessions

**Outstanding LfO Quality:**
- Clear description of who you learned from and why
- Specific insights or skills gained
- How you applied this learning to your venture/team
- Critical evaluation of what worked and what didn't
- Honest reflection on your engagement level
- Future learning needs identified

**Word Count:** ~500-600 words each

---

### Learning by Doing (LbD) - 6 Undertakings

**What They Are:**
Learning experiences where you develop skills and knowledge through direct action and experience.

**Examples:**
- Building a product or service
- Running a marketing campaign
- Managing a project or event
- Developing partnerships
- Handling finances or budgets
- Leading a team initiative

**Outstanding LbD Quality:**
- Specific actions taken (not vague descriptions)
- Results achieved (with metrics where possible)
- Challenges faced and how you overcame them
- What you learned about yourself
- Skills developed through the experience
- What you would do differently
- Connection to competencies development

**Word Count:** ~500-600 words each

## üìä UNDERTAKING STRUCTURE GUIDE

### Recommended Structure for Each Undertaking:

**1. Context (50-75 words)**
- What was the undertaking?
- Why did you choose it?
- What did you hope to learn?

**2. Action Taken (150-200 words)**
- Specific steps you took
- People involved (LfO) or actions taken (LbD)
- Timeline and duration
- Resources used

**3. Critical Evaluation (200-250 words)**
- What went well? What didn't?
- What challenges did you face?
- What would you do differently?
- Honest self-assessment of your performance

**4. Learning Achieved (100-125 words)**
- Specific skills or knowledge gained
- How this connects to your Learning Contract
- Connection to your SMART objectives
- Impact on your competencies development

**5. Future Application (50-75 words)**
- How will you apply this learning going forward?
- What further development is needed?
- Next steps

## ‚ö†Ô∏è COMMON PITFALLS

**‚ùå Not Outstanding:**
```

The speaker was knowledgeable and I took good notes. I will use this in my business.
```

**‚úÖ Outstanding:**
```
LfO Undertaking: Advanced Sales Workshop with Tom Hopkins (Oct 15)

Context: With zero partnerships and weak communication skills (Q2), I identified
sales as a critical gap. My Q4 competency development goal is "influencing stakeholders."
I chose this intensive workshop to address this gap systematically.

Action Taken: 8-hour workshop focused on consultative selling methodologies.
Learned the Sandler Selling System: question-driven approach, upfront contracts,
negative reverse selling. Practiced role-playing with 5 other entrepreneurs.
Received direct feedback from Tom on my tendency to "pitch too early."

Critical Evaluation: The workshop challenged my assumption that selling is about
persuasion. I learned it's about qualification and disqualification. My initial
role-plays were mediocre - I kept jumping to solutions instead of understanding
pain points. Tom's feedback was direct: "You're solving problems they don't have."
I struggled with the negative reverse technique but after 4 attempts, started
seeing breakthrough moments in conversations.

However, I didn't fully implement the upfront contract technique. I found it
awkward and avoided it. This is a pattern - I resist structured communication
when it feels inauthentic. I need to work on this.

Learning Achieved: Shifted from "persuasion" mindset to "qualification" mindset.
Learned to ask better questions: "What's the cost of not solving this problem?"
instead of "Would you like our solution?" Applied immediately to agency outreach -
response rate doubled from 15% to 30% when I focused on problems instead of solutions.

Direct connection to Q4 Professional Communication competency. Also improved
Strategic Leadership - I'm now teaching Sam these techniques.

Future Application: Continue practicing Sandler methodology in all partnership
conversations. Track metrics: meeting booking rate, objection handling success,
deal velocity. Plan advanced negotiation workshop for February.

Word Count: 547 words
```

## üîç HONEST REFLECTION INDICATORS

**Outstanding Undertakings Include:**
- Times you failed or struggled (not just successes)
- What you would do differently
- Resistance to feedback or new approaches
- Mistakes you made and what they taught you
- Gaps in your skills or knowledge
- Discomfort or growth moments
- Specific feedback received from others

**Example:**
```
What I'd Do Differently: I spent 3 weeks building the partnership CRM before
talking to a single agency. This was backwards - I should have done 20 manual
partnerships first to understand the workflow, then automated what actually worked.
I got seduced by building instead of selling. I recognized this pattern from my
app development failure in September. I need to force myself to validate before
building every time.
```
