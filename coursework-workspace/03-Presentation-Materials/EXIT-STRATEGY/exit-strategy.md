# Exit Strategy: AI Automation Agency

## Executive Summary

This document outlines the exit strategy for an AI automation agency targeting UK SMEs. The business has transitioned from a time-for-money development model to an AI infrastructure provider with recurring revenue. With 5 clients in the pipeline, a £1,000 paid project delivered, and a 40% discovery-to-pilot conversion rate, the business is positioned for a trade sale within 5-7 years.

The exit strategy prioritises trade sale as the primary route, with bootstrap-to-dividends and IP licensing as secondary options. Clear pivot triggers and a 3-month operational plan ensure accountability and measurable progress.

---

## 1. Business Model & Value Proposition

### 1.1 Current Model

The business operates as an AI infrastructure provider rather than a traditional code-for-hire agency:

| Component | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Setup Fee** | £1,000 (one-time) |
| **Retainer** | £200/month (recurring) |
| **Operational Margin** | 10% on client costs |
| **Key Differentiator** | System runs autonomously — not time-associated |

This model shifts the business from trading hours for pounds to building infrastructure that generates recurring value. The AI agents continuously improve client applications, meaning the service delivered improves over time without proportional cost increases.

### 1.2 Why This Model Supports Exit

- **Recurring Revenue**: Retainers create predictable cash flows that acquirers value
- **Scalable Infrastructure**: Not founder-dependent — systems do the work
- **High Margins**: 10% operational margin + low overhead = strong unit economics
- **Productised Service**: Standardised pricing and delivery reduces acquisition risk

## 4. Exit Options

### 4.1 Primary: Trade Sale

**Description:** Sell to a larger agency network or enterprise seeking AI automation capability.

**Target Buyers:**
- Digital agencies looking to add AI services
- Enterprise IT consultancies expanding into SME market
- Private equity firms acquiring automation specialists
- Trade buyers in adjacent markets

**Valuation Range:**
| Scenario | Multiple | Basis |
|----------|----------|-------|
| Conservative | 3x ARR | Standard agency multiple |
| Base Case | 4x ARR | AI premium + growth trajectory |
| Optimistic | 5x ARR | Strategic fit + IP value |

At £50K ARR, this yields £150K-250K. At £500K ARR, this yields £1.5M-2.5M.

**Timeline:** 5-7 years post-graduation

**Why This Works:**
- Current model creates recurring revenue that agencies want
- AI automation is a hot sector commanding premiums
- Client relationships are transferable assets

### 4.2 Secondary: Bootstrap to Dividends

**Description:** Scale to £500K+ ARR, then take regular dividends without selling.

**Mechanism:**
- Build to £500K ARR over 3-5 years
- Maintain 30-40% profit margins
- Extract £50-100K annually as dividends
- Continue operating indefinitely

**Why This Works:**
- No exit required — generates ongoing income
- Full control — no acquirer integration risk
- Lower risk than growth-at-all-costs

### 4.3 Tertiary: License IP

**Description:** License the agent infrastructure to other agencies or consultants.

**Potential Licensees:**
- Marketing agencies wanting AI capabilities
- Consultancies serving the same SME segment
- Freelancers wanting to offer automation services

**Model:** License fee + per-client royalty

**Why This Works:**
- Leverage existing infrastructure investment
- Passive income stream
- Lower execution burden than direct service

## 9. Conclusion

This exit strategy positions the AI automation agency for a trade sale within 5-7 years, with a clear path to £150K-250K at conservative multiples, scaling to £1.5M-2.5M if growth targets are met.

The business model — AI infrastructure with recurring retainers — creates the exact assets acquirers value: predictable revenue, scalable systems, and transferable client relationships.

The 3-month plan provides immediate accountability, while the exit triggers ensure the founder does not persist in a failing venture beyond defined deadlines.

**Key Assumptions:**
- UK SME AI market continues growth trajectory
- Business can hire and retain technical talent
- Founder remains committed for 5+ years
- No major technological disruption

**Confidence Level:** 75% — primary risk is execution speed; market and model fundamentals are sound.
