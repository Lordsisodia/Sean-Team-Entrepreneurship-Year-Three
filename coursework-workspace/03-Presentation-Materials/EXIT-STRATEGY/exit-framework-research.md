# Exit Strategy Research: AI Automation Agency

## What Investors Look For

Investors and assessors evaluate exit strategies on three fronts: **credibility of the pathway**, **realistic returns**, and **execution risk**.

**Key assessment criteria:**
- Clear, specific exit route (not vague "maybe acquisition or IPO")
- Realistic timeline (3-7 years for SMB exits)
- Evidence of buyer interest or market demand
- Founder commitment to see it through
- Financial projections tied to concrete milestones
- Understanding of what makes the business attractive to acquirers

The biggest red flag is a generic "exit strategy" copied from templates. Assessors want to see you've thought specifically about *your* business.

## Valuation Methods for Small Service/SaaS

### 1. Revenue Multiple (Most Common)
**Formula:** Valuation = Annual Revenue x Multiple

For SMB agencies and early-stage SaaS, multiples typically range from **0.5x to 3x revenue** depending on growth, margins, and scalability.

### 2. EBITDA Multiple
**Formula:** Valuation = EBITDA x Multiple

More common for profitable businesses. UK SMB agency multiples range from **3x to 7x EBITDA**.

### 3. Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)
Projects future cash flows and discounts back to present value. More rigorous but requires solid financial projections.

### 4. Asset-Based
Used for service businesses with minimal IP. Often yields lowest valuations but provides a floor.

### 5. Recurring Revenue Premium
For SaaS with subscriptions: MRR x 12-36 months. AI automation agencies with retainer contracts command premiums.

## UK SMB Agency Trade Sale Multiples

| Business Type | Typical Multiple | Notes |
|--------------|------------------|-------|
| Digital/creative agency | 2-4x revenue | Depends on client concentration |
| SaaS business | 3-8x ARR | Recurring revenue commands premium |
| AI/automation specialist | 3-6x revenue | Hot sector, strategic value |
| Consultancy | 1.5-3x revenue | Highly dependent on founders |

**Key factors affecting multiples:**
- **Recurring revenue %** - Higher = better multiple
- **Client concentration** - Diversified base = 20-30% premium
- **Team independence** - Not founder-dependent
- **IP/technology** - Proprietary tools or methodologies
- **Growth trajectory** - YoY growth > 30% = significant premium
- **Strategic fit** - Acquirer in adjacent market pays more

## Elements of a Credible Exit Strategy

1. **Specific exit routes** (pick 2-3: trade sale, MBO, secondary buyout, IPO)
2. **Target buyer profiles** - Who would buy and why? Name realistic acquirers
3. **Valuation range** - Based on comparable transactions
4. **Timeline with milestones** - What needs to happen to make exit viable
5. **Key value drivers** - What specifically makes the business worth acquiring
6. **Risk mitigation** - What's the plan if market conditions change?
7. **Founder transition** - How will you exit while ensuring business continuity?
8. **Financial preparation** - Clean books, proper contracts, IP protection
