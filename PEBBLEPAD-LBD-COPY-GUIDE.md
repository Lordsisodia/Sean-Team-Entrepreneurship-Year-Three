**Purpose:** This guide provides the exact content to copy from your LbD undertaking files into the PebblePad "Evidence of Learning" main page for each of the 6 LbD undertakings.

## Overview: What to Copy for Each LbD Undertaking

For each undertaking, you'll need to copy a **2-3 sentence summary** that captures:
1. **What you did** (the activity)
2. **What you learned** (the key insight)
3. **How it connects to your strategy** (the impact)

## LbD Undertaking 1: Recognising & Correcting Strategic Misalignment

**Copy this content to PebblePad:**

> "From September-October 2025, I spent 8 weeks building internal tools and vertical app templates instead of advancing my SMART objectives (client app, partnerships, delegation). I recognised my pattern of using technical work as avoidance - retreating to comfortable coding when facing uncertain, high-stakes activities like partnership development. This experience taught me the critical difference between 'feels productive' and 'is strategically valuable,' leading me to implement protected hours for partnership outreach and a strategic alignment check before any new work. The opportunity cost of those 8 weeks was potentially 20-30 partnership conversations, making this a foundational lesson for achieving my 1,000 partnership goal."

**Connected Competency:** Entrepreneurial Judgment
**Connected SMART Objective:** #5 (Client App MVP), #6 (100 Partnerships)

## LbD Undertaking 2: Restaurant to E-commerce Pivot - Industry Recognition Through Client Discovery

**Copy this content to PebblePad:**

> "In November 2025, I delivered an e-commerce client project (£1,000) involving 7-8 rounds of iterative feedback. The biggest lesson was that the client's understanding of their business and customers was consistently more accurate than my technical assumptions. When I suggested features I thought were 'obvious,' the client corrected me with explanations like 'My customers don't use mobile apps for that.' This project taught me that iteration isn't failure - it's the discovery process for requirements. The technical skills from restaurants transferred (payment integration, mobile app architecture), but market understanding didn't transfer - validating my strategic pivot to e-commerce where I found stronger economic viability and client willingness to pay."

**Connected Competency:** Service Design & Delivery
**Connected SMART Objective:** #5 (Client App MVP & 100% Satisfaction)

## LbD Undertaking 3: Internal Network Partnership Validation

**Copy this content to PebblePad:**

> "In December 2025, I tested partnership demand by reaching out to 25 people in my internal network with a simple value proposition: 'Partnership app ready in 2 weeks, want notified?' 17 out of 25 confirmed interest (68% response rate), demonstrating real demand before significant product investment. This experience challenged my assumption that partnership acquisition requires complex pitches and cold outreach to strangers. The key insight: 'The promise of value is more powerful than the demonstration of value' - 17 people committed based on concept and trust, not a working product. This validated the 'sell then build' approach and proved that warm network contacts convert at 4x higher rates than cold outreach, fundamentally shaping my partnership acquisition strategy."

**Connected Competency:** Professional Communication
**Connected SMART Objective:** #6 (100 Agency Partnerships by April 2026)

## LbD Undertaking 4: Tracking System Failure and Process Documentation Learning

**Copy this content to PebblePad:**

> "From December 2025 to January 2026, I attempted to delegate infrastructure setup and client acquisition to Sam, implementing a daily task tracking system with £2.49/day fines for missed tracking. After 60 days, both of us owed hundreds of pounds in fines, so we wrote off the system. The profound leadership insight: Sam successfully completed all assigned work (I never had to redo anything), proving he's capable - the tracking system was my failure, not his performance. This taught me that extrinsic motivation (financial penalties) fails when the system is poorly designed, and that good systems make the right behavior easy rather than punishing the wrong behavior. I shifted from control-based leadership to process-based leadership, now focusing on clear documentation rather than burdensome tracking."

**Connected Competency:** Strategic Leadership
**Connected SMART Objective:** #3 (Sam Managing 50+ Partnerships by March 2026)

## LbD Undertaking 5: Difficult Client Management

**Copy this content to PebblePad:**

> "In November 2025, I took on a crypto app client who was demanding, aggressive in communication, and unreliable with payments. After 3 weeks of escalating difficulty, I ended the client relationship. The biggest insight: 'Client selection happens before project commitment, not during. You can't turn a difficult client into an easy one.' I learned that early warning signs (demanding tone, unrealistic urgency, payment delays) predict future problems and don't improve with time. Difficult clients consume more than time - they consume mental energy and emotional bandwidth, creating opportunity costs with better clients. This led me to develop a client qualification framework focused on respectful communication, reasonable expectations, and payment reliability - turning down bad revenue creates space for ideal clients."

**Connected Competency:** Entrepreneurial Judgment
**Connected SMART Objective:** #5 (Client App MVP & 100% Satisfaction)

## LbD Undertaking 6: Managing Lost Opportunities and Building Pipeline Resilience

**Copy this content to PebblePad:**

> "In late November to December 2025, I pursued a construction deal with Nick Mearson that ultimately collapsed after multiple conversations. The deal faded without clear rejection rather than ending with a definitive 'no.' Initially I felt disappointed and questioned whether I was doing something wrong, but I reframed the experience: deal fallout is normal, not personal. The key insight was that relying on single deals creates vulnerability - pipeline diversification is emotional protection as much as business strategy. Resilience isn't about not feeling disappointment; it's about processing disappointment quickly and returning to action. I learned to maintain multiple conversations in parallel so that no single loss creates crisis, separating my effort (which I can control) from the result (which I can't)."

**Connected Competency:** Professional Communication
**Connected SMART Objective:** #6 (100 Agency Partnerships by April 2026)
